SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: This Act allows an owner a separate path to restore a destroyed home. (Flesch Read-
ability Score: 69.9).
Requires local governments to approve restoration or replacement of dwellings rendered
uninhabitable under an alternative process that is not a land use decision.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to restoration of dwellings damaged by involuntary causes; creating new provisions; and

5 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2025 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 197.
6 SECTION 2. (1) The local government shall approve an application to restore or replace
7 a dwelling damaged or destroyed by natural or involuntary causes if:
8 (a) The former dwelling:
9 (A) Had intact exterior walls and roof structure;
10 (B) Had indoor plumbing consisting of a kitchen sink, toilet and bathing facilities con-
11 nected to a sanitary waste disposal system;
12 (C) Had interior wiring for interior lights;
13 (D) Had a heating system; and
14 (E)(i) Was authorized by building permits or other regulatory approval process by the
15 appropriate authority; or
16 (ii) Was assessed as a residential dwelling for purposes of ad valorem taxation for the tax
17 year beginning on July 1, 10 years before the date of the application, and is not subject to
18 unresolved enforcement proceedings challenging the lawfulness of the dwelling; and
19 (b) The proposed dwelling will:
20 (A) Not exceed the floor area of the former dwelling by more than 25 percent;
21 (B) Be adequately served by water, sanitation and roads;
22 (C) Be located wholly or partially within the footprint of the former dwelling unless
23 necessary to comply with local flood regulations or to avoid a natural hazard area, in which
24 case the applicant may choose a suitable location on the same lot or parcel; and
25 (D) Comply with applicable building codes that were in effect on the later of:
26 (i) January 1, 2008; or
27 (ii) The date of the former dwelling’s construction.
