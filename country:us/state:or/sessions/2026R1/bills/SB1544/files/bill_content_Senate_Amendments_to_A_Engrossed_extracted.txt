Section 1:
SENATE AMENDMENTS TO
A-ENGROSSED SENATE BILL 1544
By JOINT COMMITTEE ON WAYS AND MEANS
February 27
1 On page 1 of the printed A-engrossed bill, line 2, after the second semicolon delete the rest of
2 the line and delete lines 3 through 5 and insert “amending ORS 184.752, 822.125 and 822.140; and
3 prescribing an effective date.”.
4 Delete lines 7 through 17 and delete pages 2 through 11 and insert:
5
6 “DISMANTLERS
7
8 “SECTION 1. ORS 822.140 is amended to read:
9 “822.140. (1) To meet the requirement for local government approval of a dismantler certificate
10 under ORS 822.110 or a supplemental certificate under ORS 822.125, an applicant must comply with
11 any regulations established by a city or county under this section and must obtain the approval of
12 the governing body of the:
13 “(a) City, if the business is or will be carried on within an incorporated city of less than 100,000
14 population.
15 “(b) County, if the business is or will be carried on outside of any incorporated city.
16 “(2) A city or county governing body shall grant approval of a dismantler certificate [or
17 renewal] or a supplemental certificate when requested under this section if the governing body:
18 “(a) Approves the applicant as being suitable to establish, maintain or operate a motor vehicle
19 dismantling business;
20 “(b) Determines that the location or proposed location meets the requirements for location under
21 ORS 822.110;
22 “(c) Determines that the location does not violate any prohibition under ORS 822.135; and
23 “(d) Approves the location and determines that the location complies with any regulations
24 adopted by a city or county under this section.
25 “(3) The governing body of a city or county may regulate the expansion of premises or the es-
26 tablishment of premises at a new location under a dismantler certificate. An applicant must comply
27 with the regulations before the Department of Transportation may issue a supplemental dismantler
28 certificate. In adopting regulations under this subsection, a governing body:
29 “(a) Shall consider the extent of development of surrounding property as a residential area;
30 “(b) Shall consider the proximity of churches, schools, hospitals, public buildings or other places
31 of public gathering;
32 “(c) Shall consider the sufficiency in number of other motor vehicle dismantling businesses in
33 the vicinity;
34 “(d) Shall consider the health, safety and general welfare of the public;
35 “(e) May establish zones in which motor vehicle dismantling businesses are permissible and
LC 279/SB 1544-A7
1 other zones where they are prohibited; and
2 “(f) May prescribe limitations on the dimensions of the premises on which motor vehicle dis-
3 mantling businesses are conducted.
4 “(4) Regulations of a city governing body that are adopted under this section apply to motor
5 vehicle dismantling businesses located outside of and within six miles of the boundaries of the city
6 unless the county governing body in which the area is located has adopted regulations under this
7 section that are applicable in the area.
8 “(5) Before granting approval for a supplemental dismantler certificate, the governing body of
9 a city or county shall notify all property owners that are or that will be adjacent to the motor ve-
10 hicle dismantling business once the business moves, expands or opens an additional place of busi-
11 ness.
12 “SECTION 2. ORS 822.125 is amended to read:
13 “822.125. (1) The holder of a current, valid dismantler certificate issued under ORS 822.110 is
14 not subject to the prohibitions and penalties under ORS 822.100 as long as the holder’s motor vehicle
15 dismantling business is conducted in the location approved under the certificate.
16 “(2) The holder of a dismantler certificate may expand the dimensions or move a place of busi-
17 ness approved under the dismantler certificate or open an additional place of business under the
18 certificate upon issuance of a supplemental dismantler certificate by the Department of Transpor-
19 tation. The following apply to supplemental certificates issued under this subsection:
20 “(a) The department shall grant a supplemental certificate upon request of an applicant under
21 this subsection if the applicant obtains local government permission for the supplemental certificate
22 under ORS 822.140.
23 “[(b) Upon application for renewal of the supplemental certificate, the department may waive the
24 requirement that an applicant for renewal under this subsection obtain local government approval un-
25 der ORS 822.140 of the suitability of the applicant to establish, maintain or operate a motor vehicle
26 dismantling business.]
27 “[(c)] (b) A fee shall be charged for a supplemental dismantler certificate under ORS 822.700.
28 “(3) A dismantler certificate is valid for a two-year period and may be renewed as provided by
29 the department. The department shall only renew the certificate of any certificate holder who does
30 all of the following:
31 “(a) Pays the required fee for renewal under ORS 822.700.
32 “(b) Completes the application described in ORS 822.115.
33 “[(c) Obtains local government approval under ORS 822.140. The department may waive the re-
34 quirement that an applicant for renewal obtain local government approval under ORS 822.140 of the
35 suitability of the applicant to establish, maintain or operate a motor vehicle dismantling business.]
36 “[(d)] (c) Maintains a current bond that meets the requirements under ORS 822.120.
37 “(4) The department may provide the holder of a dismantler certificate with identification cards
38 in the names of the owners of the business or in the names of authorized employees of the business.
39 “(5) The department may adopt suitable rules for the issuance and renewal of dismantler cer-
40 tificates and identification cards.
41
42 “PUBLIC TRANSPORTATION PROVIDERS
43
44 “SECTION 3. ORS 184.752 is amended to read:
45 “184.752. As used in ORS 184.752 to 184.766:
SA to A-Eng. SB 1544 Page 2
1 “(1) ‘Public transportation service provider’ includes a qualified entity and a city, county, special
2 district, intergovernmental entity or any other political subdivision or municipal or public corpo-
3 ration that provides public transportation services and is not otherwise described in subsection (2)
4 of this section.
5 “(2) ‘Qualified entity’ means the following:
6 “(a)(A) Counties in which no part of a mass transit district or transportation district exists; or
7 “(B) Intergovernmental entities who are providing public transportation services on be-
8 half of a county;
9 “(b) Mass transit districts organized under ORS 267.010 to 267.394;
10 “(c) Transportation districts organized under ORS 267.510 to 267.650; and
11 “(d)(A) Federally recognized Indian tribes; or
12 “(B) Intergovernmental entities who are providing public transportation services on be-
13 half of a tribe.
14
15 “CAPTIONS
16
17 “SECTION 4. The unit captions used in this 2026 Act are provided only for the conven-
18 ience of the reader and do not become part of the statutory law of this state or express any
19 legislative intent in the enactment of this 2026 Act.
20
21 “EFFECTIVE DATE
22
23 “SECTION 5. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
24 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.”.
