SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: This Act prohibits state public bodies from helping the feds sell their public lands.
(Flesch Readability Score: 69.9).
Prohibits public bodies from assisting the federal government with privatization of certain
federally owned lands.
Sunsets January 2, 2032.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to public lands; and declaring an emergency.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. (1) A public body, as defined in ORS 174.109, may not use any moneys, data,
5 technology, equipment, personnel or other resources or enter into any agreement for the
6 purpose of assisting the federal government or any agency of the federal government with
7 the sale or transfer of real property in this state to a private person not including a public
8 body, federally recognized Indian tribe in Oregon or entity owned by a public body or tribe.
9 (2) This section applies only to real property that is, on the effective date of this 2026
10 Act, managed or administered by:
11 (a) The United States Bureau of Land Management;
12 (b) The United States Fish and Wildlife Service;
13 (c) The United States Forest Service; or
14 (d) The National Park Service of the United States Department of the Interior.
15 (3) This section does not apply to real property that:
16 (a) Is within an urban growth boundary, as defined in ORS 197.015;
17 (b) Is being transferred for a specific infrastructure, utility, transportation, conservation
18 or recreational use;
19 (c) Consists primarily of one or more surplus federal buildings;
20 (d) Is held in trust or for the benefit of a federally recognized Indian tribe in Oregon or
21 a member of a tribe;
22 (e) Is being transferred in exchange for comparable private lands for the primary purpose
23 of consolidating public or private land for management;
24 (f) Is acquired by the Department of State Lands in lieu of lands granted under the Act
