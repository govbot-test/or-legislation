Section 1:
HB 4007-14
(LC 255)
2/4/26 (HE/ps)
Requested by Representative MANNIX
PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO
1 On page 10 of the printed bill, delete lines 17 through 39 and insert:
2 “SECTION 13. ORS 814.486 is amended to read:
3 “814.486. (1) A person commits the offense of endangering a [bicycle] ve-
4 hicle operator or passenger if:
5 “(a) The person is operating or using a [bicycle] vehicle on a highway
6 or on premises open to the public and the person carries another person on
7 the [bicycle] vehicle who is under 16 years of age and is not wearing pro-
8 tective headgear of a type approved under ORS 815.052; or
9 “(b) The person is the parent, legal guardian or person with legal re-
10 sponsibility for the safety and welfare of a child under 16 years of age and
11 the child operates, uses or rides on a [bicycle] vehicle on a highway or on
12 premises open to the public without wearing protective headgear of a type
13 approved under ORS 815.052.
14 “[(2) Exemptions from this section are as provided in ORS 814.487.]
15 “(2) This section applies to the following vehicles:
16 “(a) Bicycles;
17 “(b) Motor assisted scooters;
18 “(c) Electric personal assistive mobility devices;
19 “(d) Powered micromobility devices; or
20 “(e) Skateboards, nonmotorized scooters or in-line skates.
21 “(3) The requirement to wear protective headgear under this section
1 does not apply if wearing the headgear would violate a religious belief
2 or practice of the person.
3 “[(3)] (4) The offense described in this section, endangering a [bicycle]
4 vehicle operator or passenger, is a specific fine traffic violation. The
5 presumptive fine for endangering a [bicycle] vehicle operator or passenger
6 is $25.
7 “(5) The first time a person is convicted of an offense described in
8 this section the person is not required to pay a fine if the person
9 proves to the satisfaction of the court that the person has protective
10 headgear of a type approved under ORS 815.052.”.
11 On page 18, line 34, delete “26” and insert “26a”.
12
