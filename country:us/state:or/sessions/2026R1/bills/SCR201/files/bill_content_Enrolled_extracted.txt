83rd OREGON LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY--2026 Regular Session
Enrolled
Senate Concurrent Resolution 201
Printed pursuant to Senate Interim Rule 213.28 by order of the President of the Senate in conform-
ance with presession filing rules, indicating neither advocacy nor opposition on the part of the
President (at the request of Senate Interim Committee on Veterans, Emergency Management,
Federal and World Affairs)
Whereas the United States Postal Service (USPS) marked the 250th anniversary of its founding
on July 26, 2025; and
Whereas Postmaster General David Steiner remarked, “In 1775, our Founding Fathers recog-
nized the importance of creating an institution that could bind the nation together and foster secure
and accessible communications between every community in America. The rich and enduring his-
tory of the Postal Service demonstrates the strength of their wisdom, and we look forward to pro-
viding reliable, affordable and universal service for generations to come.”; and
Whereas USPS was founded in 1775, and it evolved as the Second Continental Congress estab-
lished post offices and postal roads and ultimately passed the Post Office Act of 1792; and
Whereas under the guidance of the first appointed Postmaster General, Benjamin Franklin,
USPS became a hallmark of the American principles of freedom of the press, right to privacy and
expanding our nation’s infrastructure; and
Whereas USPS is a permanent fixture of the federal government, delivering mail to nearly 168
million addresses in the United States; and
Whereas covering every state, city and town, USPS serves as the largest physical and logistical
infrastructure of any nonmilitary government institution in the United States; and
Whereas on March 9, 1847, the first USPS-authorized post office west of the Rocky Mountains
was established in Astoria under Oregon’s first postmaster, John M. Shively, and a second post of-
fice followed in Oregon City in 1848 under postmaster Egbert Bennett; and
Whereas those first post offices in Astoria and Oregon City served as crucial hubs for the de-
livery of mail across the country beginning in 1849; and
Whereas USPS is among the most diverse organizations in the nation, with 45 percent of its
roughly 640,000-strong workforce made up of women, 54 percent made up of historically underrep-
resented minorities, more than 70,000 employees who are veterans of the United States Armed
Forces and more than 42,000 employees with disabilities; and
Whereas approximately 8-1/2 percent of the USPS workforce, or roughly 5,500 employees, are
based in Oregon, including roughly 4,000 letter carriers; and
Whereas Oregonians take great pride in this state’s deep history with, and enduring connections
to, the United States Postal Service; now, therefore,
Be It Resolved by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Oregon:
That we, the members of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly, honor the United States Postal
Service for its rich history of connecting people, communities and commerce; and be it further
Resolved, That we thank all letter carriers and USPS employees for their dedication,
professionalism and commitment to excellence; and be it further
Resolved, That we celebrate 250 years of collecting, sorting, processing, transporting and deliv-
ering our nation’s mail.
