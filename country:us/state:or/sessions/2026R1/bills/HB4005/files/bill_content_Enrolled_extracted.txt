AN ACT
Relating to Water Professionals Appreciation Week; and prescribing an effective date.
Whereas water is the lifeblood of Oregon, and no community or sector of the economy, from
agriculture to manufacturing to tourism, can thrive or grow without safe and reliable access to
water; and
Whereas reliable and effective delivery of public drinking water and sanitary sewer services is
essential to protecting public health, to supporting community growth, to the production of housing,
to livability and to the efficient operation of safety services, including critical fire protection ser-
vices; and
Whereas technological advances by Oregon’s thousands of water professionals have made this
state’s public drinking water and treated wastewater meet some of the most stringent water quality
standards in the United States; and
Whereas water professionals are essential stewards of our state’s natural environment, working
diligently to protect both in-stream and out-of-stream water resources; and
Whereas through careful management of water withdrawals, treatment processes and discharge
practices, water professionals help maintain healthy river and stream ecosystems, support fish and
wildlife habitats and safeguard water quality for downstream users; and
Whereas the tireless dedication of Oregon’s water professionals ensures that environmental
protections are balanced with community needs, advancing practices that promote conservation,
sustainability and the long-term resilience of Oregon’s watersheds; and
Whereas regardless of where it originates, drinking water from public water systems is filtered,
cleaned, tested and distributed in a process carefully managed by water system operators certified
by the Oregon Health Authority; and
Whereas approximately 77 percent of Oregonians are actively served by public water systems
that deliver safe drinking water each day through an extensive network of pipelines and distribution
systems; and
Whereas hundreds of millions of gallons of wastewater are managed each day through thousands
of miles of sanitary sewers and more than 300 domestic wastewater treatment plants in Oregon,
protecting both public health and the environment in a process carefully managed by wastewater
operators certified by the Department of Environmental Quality; and
Whereas Oregon’s water professionals utilize innovative approaches to address both the state’s
decreasing water supply and impacts on water quality due to environmental changes, including sig-
nificantly expanding the reuse of treated wastewater; and
Whereas water professionals at local public drinking water and wastewater agencies work 24
hours a day, seven days a week, including on holidays, to protect public health and the environment,

to plan for the future of our most essential resource, to maintain and upgrade crucial water storage,
filtering and delivery systems and to improve the safety and resiliency of local water supplies; and
Whereas the League of Oregon Cities, the Special Districts Association of Oregon, the Oregon
Association of Clean Water Agencies, WateReuse Pacific Northwest, the Oregon Water Utility
Council and the Oregon Association of Water Utilities all acknowledge and celebrate the essential
work of the water professionals employed by their agency members; now, therefore,
Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
SECTION 1. (1) The first Saturday of October of each year through the end of the Sunday
of the following weekend is designated as Water Professionals Appreciation Week.
(2) The purpose of the designation is to:
(a) Encourage public water agencies and wastewater agencies to offer tours, open houses
and other events to educate Oregonians on the important functions of those agencies; and
(b) Encourage all Oregonians to learn about the water and wastewater industries in
Oregon and consider careers in this critical field.
SECTION 2. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.
