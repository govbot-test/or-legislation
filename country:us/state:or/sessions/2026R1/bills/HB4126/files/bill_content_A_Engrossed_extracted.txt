Section 1:
A-Engrossed
House Bill 4126
Ordered by the House February 18
Including House Amendments dated February 18
Sponsored by Representatives GAMBA, RIEKE SMITH; Representatives ANDERSEN, MUNOZ, Senators
FREDERICK, GOLDEN, MEEK, NERON MISSLIN, PHAM K (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act would have ODOT biennially recommend a rate for the per-mile road usage
charge that would raise the money needed to keep up the highways in this state. (Flesch Readability
Score: 60.7).
Requires the Department of Transportation to submit a biennial report to the Legislative As-
sembly recommending a rate for the per-mile road usage charge that would sustainably raise the
revenue necessary to maintain the public highways in this state.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to road usage charges; and declaring an emergency.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS 319.883 to
5 319.946.
6 SECTION 2. (1) Not later than September 15 of each even-numbered year, the Depart-
7 ment of Transportation shall submit a report, in the manner provided in ORS 192.245, to the
8 interim committees of the Legislative Assembly related to transportation, that recommends
9 a rate for the per-mile road usage charge that would sustainably raise the revenue necessary
10 to maintain the public highways of this state.
11 (2) The report must include:
12 (a) The total estimated cost for maintenance and preservation of Oregon’s transportation
13 system, including for local systems; and
14 (b) An estimate of the total vehicle miles driven on the public highways of this state
15 based on the most recently available data on vehicle miles traveled, set forth separately ac-
16 cording to the following weight table:
17 (A) Vehicles weighing 10,000 pounds or less.
18 (B) Vehicles weighing more than 10,000 pounds and not more than 12,000 pounds.
19 (C) Vehicles weighing more than 12,000 pounds and not more than 14,000 pounds.
20 (D) Vehicles weighing more than 14,000 pounds and not more than 16,000 pounds.
21 (E) Vehicles weighing more than 16,000 pounds and not more than 18,000 pounds.
22 (F) Vehicles weighing more than 18,000 pounds and not more than 20,000 pounds.
23 (G) Vehicles weighing more than 20,000 pounds and not more than 22,000 pounds.
24 (H) Vehicles weighing more than 22,000 pounds and not more than 24,000 pounds.
25 (I) Vehicles weighing more than 24,000 pounds and not more than 26,000 pounds.

New sections are in boldfaced type.

5 (a) The desired pavement condition index for each road type;
