SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: The Act would set a maximum percent required for matching grants made by the state
to small cities for capital projects. (Flesch Readability Score: 64.6).
Sets maximum matching fund requirements for state grants to small incorporated cities for
capital construction and municipal infrastructure.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to matching grants for cities; and prescribing an effective date.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. (1) Matching grants for capital construction and municipal infrastructure
5 made by the State of Oregon to incorporated cities with a population not greater than 20,000
6 must be offered on the following terms, based on population according to the most recent
7 federal decennial census:
8 (a) Incorporated cities with a population not greater than 5,000 may be required to pro-
9 vide matching funds of not greater than:
10 (A) Three percent of the amount of a grant award of $100,000 or less.
11 (B) Five percent of the amount of a grant award greater than $100,000.
12 (b) Incorporated cities with a population greater than 5,000 but not greater than 7,500
13 may be required to provide matching funds of not greater than seven percent of the amount
14 of the grant award.
15 (c) Incorporated cities with a population greater than 7,500 but not greater than 10,000
16 may be required to provide matching funds of not greater than nine percent of the amount
17 of the grant award.
18 (d) Incorporated cities with a population greater than 10,000 but not greater than 20,000
19 may be required to provide matching funds of not greater than 12 percent of the amount of
20 the grant award.
21 (2) Incorporated cities with a population not greater than 20,000 may use grant moneys
22 described in subsection (1) of this section for all phases of a funded project in addition to
23 construction, including, but not limited to, engineering, planning and support.
24 SECTION 2. Section 1 of this 2026 Act applies to matching grants awarded to incorpo-
25 rated cities on or after the effective date of this 2026 Act.
26 SECTION 3. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
27 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.
