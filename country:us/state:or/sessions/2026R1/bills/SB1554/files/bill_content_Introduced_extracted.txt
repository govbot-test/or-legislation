Section 1:
Senate Bill 1554
Sponsored by Senators LINTHICUM, SMITH DB; Senators ANDERSON, NASH, ROBINSON, THATCHER, WEBER
(Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: The Act requires that a doctor provide the same standard of care to any child born alive
that requires life-saving treatment. (Flesch Readability Score: 62.6).
Requires a health care practitioner to exercise the proper degree of care to preserve the health
and life of a child born alive, regardless of whether the birth was the result of an induced abortion.
Allows specified persons to bring an action against a health care practitioner for violations.
Allows the court to order that the identity or personally identifiable information of specified
persons is protected from disclosure.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to infants born alive; creating new provisions; and amending ORS 677.190.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. Sections 2 to 7 of this 2026 Act shall be known and may be cited as the
5 Born-Alive Infants Protection Act.
6 SECTION 2. As used in sections 2 to 7 of this 2026 Act:
7 (1) “Abortion” means the use or prescription of a drug, medicine, instrument or other
8 substance or device in order to:
9 (a) Intentionally kill the unborn child of a pregnant person; or
10 (b) Terminate a person’s pregnancy with a purpose other than:
11 (A) To produce a live birth and preserve the life and health of the child; or
12 (B) To remove a dead unborn child.
13 (2) “Born alive” means the complete expulsion or extraction of a child from a person at
14 any stage of the child’s development and after the expulsion or extraction, the child is
15 breathing or has a beating heart, pulsation of the umbilical cord or definite movement of
16 voluntary muscles, whether the umbilical cord has been severed or not. Expulsion or ex-
17 traction may occur by natural or induced labor, cesarean section or induced abortion.
18 (3) “Health care practitioner” means a person who is authorized by a health professional
19 regulatory agency in this state to engage in the practice of a health care discipline.
20 SECTION 3. For purposes of determining the meaning of any statute, rule or regulation,
21 or interpretation thereof by an agency of this state, the terms “person,” “human being,”
22 “child” and “individual” include an infant member of the species Homo sapiens who is born
23 alive at any stage of the member’s development.
24 SECTION 4. (1) When a child is born alive and requires lifesaving treatment to survive,
25 regardless of whether the child’s birth occurred by natural or induced labor, cesarean section
26 or induced abortion, any health care practitioner who is present at the time the child is born
27 alive shall:

New sections are in boldfaced type.

1 (a) Exercise the same degree of professional skill, care and diligence to preserve the life
2 and health of the child that a reasonably diligent and conscientious health care practitioner
3 would render to any other child born alive at the same gestational age; and
4 (b) Following the exercise of skill, care and diligence required under paragraph (a) of this
5 subsection, if the child was not born alive in a hospital, ensure that the child born alive is
6 immediately transported and admitted to a hospital. If the child was born alive at a hospital,
7 the health care practitioner shall continue to exercise the skill, care and diligence required
8 under paragraph (a) of this subsection.
9 (2) A health care practitioner, or an employee of a health care clinic where a pregnancy
10 results in a child born alive, who has knowledge of a violation of subsection (1) of this section
11 shall immediately report the violation to a state law enforcement agency.
12 SECTION 5. Intentional or reckless failure by a health care practitioner to meet the re-
13 quirements of section 4 of this 2026 Act:
14 (1) Constitutes unprofessional conduct for purposes of ORS 677.190.
15 (2) May be the basis for disciplinary action under ORS 678.111.
16 SECTION 6. (1)(a) Except as provided in paragraph (b) of this subsection, a person whose
17 pregnancy resulted in a child born alive who required lifesaving treatment to survive but who
18 was not provided with the standard of care required under section 4 of this 2026 Act, or the
19 person responsible for the fertilization that resulted in the pregnancy, may maintain an
20 action against the health care practitioner who failed to provide the standard of care re-
21 quired by section 4 of this 2026 Act for actual and punitive damages.
22 (b) Damages may not be awarded under this subsection to the person responsible for the
23 fertilization that resulted in the pregnancy described in this subsection if the pregnancy was
24 the result of criminal conduct by the person responsible for the fertilization.
25 (2) A cause of action for injunctive relief against a health care practitioner who inten-
26 tionally or recklessly violates section 4 of this 2026 Act may be brought against the health
27 care practitioner by:
28 (a) A person whose pregnancy resulted in the child born alive;
29 (b) The representative of a person whose pregnancy resulted in the child born alive if the
30 person is deceased;
31 (c) The person responsible for the fertilization that resulted in the pregnancy;
32 (d) A prosecuting attorney with competent jurisdiction; or
33 (e) The Attorney General.
34 (3) Injunctive relief may not be granted to the person responsible for fertilization that
35 resulted in the pregnancy if the pregnancy is the result of criminal conduct by the person
36 described in this subsection.
37 (4) If the plaintiff prevails in the action described in this section, the court shall also
38 award reasonable attorney fees to the plaintiff.
39 SECTION 7. (1) In any action or proceeding brought under section 6 of this 2026 Act, the
40 court shall determine whether the anonymity of a person whose pregnancy resulted in the
41 child born alive must be exempt from public disclosure under ORS 192.311 to 192.478 if the
42 person does not consent to disclosure.
43 (2) If the court determines that the person’s anonymity must be preserved, the court
44 shall issue orders to the parties, witnesses and counsel, direct the sealing of the record and
45 order exclusion from the courtroom or hearing room of all persons who are not parties,
[2]

1 witnesses or counsel. The orders described in this subsection must be accompanied by writ-
2 ten findings that explain why the person’s anonymity must be preserved, why the order is
3 essential to that end, how the order is narrowly tailored to serve that interest and why no
4 reasonable less-restrictive alternative exists.
5 (3) If the person described in subsection (1) of this section does not consent to disclose
6 the person’s identity, any person other than a public official who brings an action under
7 section 6 of this 2026 Act that is related to the person’s pregnancy shall bring the action
8 under a pseudonym.
9 (4) This section may not be construed to conceal the identity of the plaintiff or witnesses
10 from the defendant or defendant’s counsel.
