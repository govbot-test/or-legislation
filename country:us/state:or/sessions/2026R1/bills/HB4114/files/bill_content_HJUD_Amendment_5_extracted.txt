PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO

1 On page 1 of the printed bill, delete lines 4 through 23 and delete pages
2 2 through 4 and insert:
3 “SECTION 1. (1) As used in this section, ‘property’ means:
4 “(a) Buildings, structures, land and curtilage.
5 “(b) Vehicles located on private property or in a location where the
6 owner of the vehicle has a reasonable expectation of privacy.
7 “(c) A church, synagogue, temple, mosque, chapel, meeting house
8 or other nonresidential place of worship.
9 “(d) A school or other educational facility.
10 “(e) A facility that provides medical treatment.
11 “(f) Any place where a person has a reasonable expectation of pri-
12 vacy.
13 “(2) An owner or occupant of property, or a person lawfully present
14 on property with a reasonable expectation of privacy, may bring a
15 cause of action against an individual who under color of law enters the
16 property without a warrant for which the issuing authority is a court,
17 if a judicially recognized exception to the warrant requirement does
18 not apply.
19 “(3) A plaintiff who prevails in a cause of action under this section
20 may recover actual economic and noneconomic damages, equitable
21 relief and any other appropriate relief.

1 “(4)(a) In an action brought under this section, the court shall
2 award reasonable attorney fees and costs to a prevailing plaintiff. In
3 an action for injunctive relief under this section, the court shall deem
4 a plaintiff to have prevailed if the plaintiff’s suit was a substantial
5 factor or significant catalyst in obtaining the results sought by the
6 litigation.
7 “(b) The court may award reasonable attorney fees and costs to a
8 defendant in an action under this section for defending any claims the
9 court finds frivolous.
10 “(5) A person may not bring an action under this section against a
11 person subject to suit for the injury under 42 U.S.C. 1983, including
12 but not limited to a law enforcement agency or an officer, as those
13 terms are defined in ORS 181A.822.
14 “(6) An action under this section must be commenced within two
15 years after the cause of action accrues.
16 “SECTION 2. This 2026 Act being necessary for the immediate
17 preservation of the public peace, health and safety, an emergency is
18 declared to exist, and this 2026 Act takes effect on its passage.”.
