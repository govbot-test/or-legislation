SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: Requires a covered platform to have an agreement in order to access the online content
of a news provider. Creates a group for civic information to give grants. (Flesch Readability Score:
62.7).
Prohibits an online news aggregating platform from accessing for an Oregon audience the online
content of a digital journalism provider without an agreement. Specifies requirements that an
agreement must meet.
Creates a private cause of action that a digital journalism provider may bring against a platform
if the platform accesses for an Oregon audience the online content of the digital journalism provider
without an agreement or fails to comply with an agreement. Allows for the recovery of the greater
of statutory or actual damages, and for punitive damages.
Bars a cause of action if the platform is participating in arbitration or in compliance with a
final arbitration order, judgment or settlement agreement. Bars a cause of action for access that
occurs between January 1, 2027, and December 31, 2028, if the platform elects to pay a certain sum
amount.
Allows a platform or group of digital journalism providers to initiate an arbitration process to
determine a percentage of the platform’s advertising revenue to be remitted to the digital journalism
providers.
Requires platforms to report annually certain information about compensation paid to digital
journalism providers.
Establishes the Oregon Civic Information Consortium. Directs the consortium to award grants
for applicants that seek to improve civic information.

16 SECTION 2. Accessing online content of digital journalism provider; agreement required.
17 (1) A covered platform may not access for an Oregon audience the online content of a digital
18 journalism provider unless the covered platform and digital journalism provider have an
19 agreement that meets the requirements of subsection (2) of this section and the covered
20 platform is in compliance with the agreement.
21 (2) An agreement between a covered platform and a digital journalism provider that al-
22 lows for the covered platform to access for an Oregon audience the online content of the
23 digital journalism provider must meet the following requirements:
24 (a) Be in writing;
25 (b) Be a standalone agreement that covers the compensation to be paid by the covered
26 platform in exchange for accessing for an Oregon audience the online content of the digital
27 journalism provider;
28 (c) Require the covered platform to compensate the digital journalism provider for ac-
29 cessing for an Oregon audience the online content of the digital journalism provider and
30 specify the amount or rate of the compensation;
31 (d) May not permit the covered platform to provide a service or added value in lieu of
32 paying compensation for accessing for an Oregon audience the online content of the digital
33 journalism provider;
34 (e) May not be conditioned on or tied to any other products or services;
35 (f) Provide for an audit of the covered platform’s access of the online content of the
36 digital journalism provider; and
37 (g) Prohibit the covered platform from selling or licensing to a third party:
38 (A) The online content of the digital journalism provider that the covered platform ac-
39 cesses; or
40 (B) Any data the covered platform collects or maintains in accessing the online content
41 of the digital journalism provider.
