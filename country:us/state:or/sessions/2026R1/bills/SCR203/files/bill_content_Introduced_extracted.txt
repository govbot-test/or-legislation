Section 1:
Senate Concurrent Resolution 203
Sponsored by Senators WAGNER, FREDERICK, Representative GRAYBER, Senators GORSEK, MANNING JR,
Representatives NELSON, SANCHEZ; Senators GELSER BLOUIN, GOLDEN, JAMA, PHAM K, PROZANSKI,
Representatives GAMBA, GOMBERG, ISADORE, NGUYEN D, PHAM H, WISE (at the request of Senator Lew
Frederick) (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: Pays respect to the life and memory of Moses Ross. (Flesch Readability Score: 78.2).
In memoriam: Moses Ross, 1966-2025.
1 CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 Whereas Moses Ross was born on June 20, 1966; and
3 Whereas at an early age Moses Ross learned from the example of his hardworking grandparents,
4 immigrants from Mexico to the mid-Willamette Valley, that “as parents, you do what is necessary
5 to provide for your family and strive to ensure the American dream for your progeny and for your
6 community”; and
7 Whereas Moses Ross attended schools in Utah, Wyoming and Ontario, Canada, before making
8 his home in Portland in the late 1980s; and
9 Whereas in 1989 Moses Ross began working for the Lake Oswego Review, and that experience
10 motivated him to start his own publication, a 50,000-circulation quarterly magazine for the local li-
11 censed holistic healthcare community that ran from 1990 to 1995; and
12 Whereas spurred on by his grandparents’ example, Moses Ross was indeed a fierce advocate for
13 his communities of Multnomah Village and greater Southwest Portland, and was known affection-
14 ately by those for whom he fought so tirelessly as the “Mayor of Multnomah Village”; and
15 Whereas as President of the Multnomah Neighborhood Association, Moses Ross proudly estab-
16 lished the first tiny pod village in Southwest Portland, providing a port in a storm for the
17 community’s houseless population; and
18 Whereas Moses Ross was an engaged and accomplished small business owner, operating the
19 nationally recognized and award winning political organizations Smart Voter Contact and
20 PoliticalRobocalls.com; and
21 Whereas in 2010, the magazine Campaigns & Elections named Moses Ross and
22 PoliticalRobocalls.com as the recipient of a prestigious Reed Award, recognizing his excellence in
23 political campaigning; and
24 Whereas Moses Ross was also a repeat winner of the American Association of Political
25 Consultants’ “Pollie” Award, honoring excellence in polling and voter engagement; and
26 Whereas Moses Ross was again recognized as a Reed Award winner in 2013 for Best Overall
27 Use of a Telephone Town Hall for Washington State Representative Larry Seaquist; and
28 Whereas Moses Ross delivered frequent electoral wins and robust voter engagement for con-
29 gressional and statewide campaigns, school board candidates, city councilors, county commissioners,
NOTE: Matter in boldfaced type in an amended section is new; matter [italic and bracketed] is existing law to be omitted.
New sections are in boldfaced type.

1 state legislators, labor unions and nonprofits in Oregon and across the nation; and
2 Whereas Moses Ross was also deeply respected for his volunteer work within the Democratic
3 Party of Oregon, where he cofounded the Democratic Party of Oregon Latino Caucus and served
4 as a delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 2008, 2016, 2020 and 2024; and
5 Whereas as a steadfast and outspoken advocate for election reform, Moses Ross served on the
6 Multnomah County Charter Review Committee, which laid the groundwork for the successful City
7 of Portland Honest Elections campaign (Measure 26-200), securing more than 87 percent approval
8 from Portland voters; and
9 Whereas Moses Ross was committed to local news, highlighting community issues as a host of
10 PDX Progressive Talk Radio on KBOO Community Radio and at the Willamette Falls Media Center;
11 and
12 Whereas whenever there was an opportunity to fight for a cause, Moses Ross showed up, making
13 calls, walking turf, marching in parades and working tables; and
14 Whereas Moses Ross always endeavored to find common ground and workable solutions for
15 Oregon’s most challenging issues, and his approach to each and every one of them was built on the
16 foundations of equity, equality and accessibility; and
17 Whereas Moses Ross was remembered by Willamette Week for his effective volunteer work, his
18 relentless positivity and kindness and his unrelenting advocacy for all those who call Portland home;
19 and
20 Whereas whether running for office, taking on new community or political responsibilities, ex-
21 panding his business or coordinating neighborhood projects, Moses Ross dedicated himself to making
22 the world a better place for all; and
23 Whereas despite his unrelenting involvement in politics and in his communities, Moses Ross was
24 also a dedicated single father and best friend to his cherished daughter, Emma, and he strove above
25 all to teach her that one person truly can make a difference; and
26 Whereas Emma remembers her dad as a loving, nerdy, amazing, one-of-a-kind person who was
27 “everything good”; and
28 Whereas Moses Ross passed away at his home in Multnomah Village at the tender age of 58 on
29 April 23, 2025; now, therefore,
30 Be It Resolved by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Oregon:
31 That we, the members of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly, honor and celebrate the life and
32 legacy of Moses Ross, and we recognize him as a force for good whose dedication improved the lives
33 of many across our state; and be it further
34 Resolved, That we recognize Moses Ross as the honorary “Mayor Emeritus of Multnomah Vil-
35 lage”; and be it further
36 Resolved, That a copy of this resolution shall be presented to Emma Ross as an expression of
37 our sympathy and condolences.
