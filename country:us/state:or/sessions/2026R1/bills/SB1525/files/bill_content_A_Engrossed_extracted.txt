Section 1:
A-Engrossed
Senate Bill 1525
Ordered by the Senate February 18
Including Senate Amendments dated February 18
Printed pursuant to Senate Interim Rule 213.28 by order of the President of the Senate in conformance with pre-
session filing rules, indicating neither advocacy nor opposition on the part of the President (at the request
of Senate Interim Committee on Energy and Environment)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: This Act creates the Blue Economy Task Force and lets the Oregon Ocean Science Trust
form a nonprofit. (Flesch Readability Score: 66.3).
Establishes the Blue Economy Task Force. Directs the task force to study Oregon’s blue econ-
omy and submit a report to the Legislative Assembly no later than September 15, 2027.
Authorizes the Oregon Ocean Science Trust to create and maintain a nonprofit organization.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to organizations relevant to Oregon’s coastal resources; and prescribing an effective date.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4
5 BLUE ECONOMY TASK FORCE
6
7 SECTION 1. (1) The Blue Economy Task Force is established.
8 (2) The task force consists of no fewer than 13 members appointed as follows:
9 (a)(A) The President of the Senate shall appoint one member from among the members
10 of the Senate.
11 (B) The Speaker of the House of Representatives shall appoint one member from among
12 members of the House of Representatives.
13 (C) Of the members appointed under this paragraph, at least one shall be a member of
14 the coastal caucus.
15 (b) The President and the Speaker shall jointly appoint not fewer than 10 members to
16 represent:
17 (A) Public and private research institutions;
18 (B) Businesses innovating in blue economy sectors;
19 (C) Commercial and noncommercial fishery businesses;
20 (D) Recreational activities other than fishing;
21 (E) The tourism industry;
22 (F) Environmental organizations;
23 (G) Equity investors in blue economy businesses;
24 (H) Coastal labor;
25 (I) The Sea Grant College of Oregon State University; and
NOTE: Matter in boldfaced type in an amended section is new; matter [italic and bracketed] is existing law to be omitted.
New sections are in boldfaced type.
LC 219
A-Eng. SB 1525
1 (J) Federally recognized Indian tribes located on the Oregon coast.
2 (c) The chair of the Ocean Policy Advisory Council, or the chair’s designee, shall serve
3 as a member of the task force.
4 (3)(a) The task force shall study and prepare a report on:
5 (A) The sectors of Oregon’s blue economy;
6 (B) Existing economic development plans or strategies for the blue economy; and
7 (C) Opportunities for the State of Oregon to facilitate or enhance the growth of
8 sustainable blue economy businesses while preserving and protecting Oregon’s coastal
9 ecosystems.
10 (b) The report must include:
11 (A) Identification and synthesis of existing state or regional studies, market analyses and
12 federal and state programs relevant to the blue economy in Oregon.
13 (B) Identification of existing state and local initiatives, including workforce programs,
14 efforts by research centers, efforts by ports, grant programs and permitting pathways, to
15 determine where existing efforts overlap, could be expanded to fill in gaps and present op-
16 portunities to be integrated into a unified strategic plan.
17 (C) A review and evaluation of efforts conducted by other states or countries to identify
18 best practices and policies to build sustainable innovation in the blue economy.
19 (D) Prioritized recommendations specific to subject areas that include policy, investment,
20 workforce development, research and development and regulatory coordination.
21 (E) A roadmap for implementing the recommendations of the task force that may include
22 the establishment of a center for the blue economy.
23 (4) A majority of the members of the task force constitutes a quorum for the transaction
24 of business.
25 (5) Official action by the task force requires the approval of a majority of the members
26 of the task force.
27 (6) The President and the Speaker shall jointly select one member of the task force to
28 serve as chairperson and another to serve as vice chairperson, for the terms and with the
29 duties and powers necessary for the performance of the functions of the offices as the
30 President and the Speaker determine.
31 (7) If there is a vacancy for any cause, the appointing authority shall make an appoint-
32 ment to become immediately effective.
33 (8) The task force shall meet at times and places specified by the call of the chairperson
34 or of a majority of the members of the task force.
35 (9) The task force may adopt rules necessary for the operation of the task force.
36 (10) The task force shall submit a report and may include recommendations for legis-
37 lation to the interim committees of the Legislative Assembly related to the environment, in
38 the manner provided by ORS 192.245, and to the Ocean Policy Advisory Council, no later than
39 September 15, 2027.
40 (11)(a) The Legislative Policy and Research Office shall provide staff support to the task
41 force.
42 (b) The task force, through the office, may contract for services necessary to carry out
43 the duties and functions of the task force.
44 (12) Members of the task force who are not members of the Legislative Assembly serve
45 as volunteers on the task force and, unless they are qualified members, as defined in ORS
[2]
A-Eng. SB 1525
1 292.495, are not entitled to compensation or reimbursement for expenses.
2 (13) All agencies of state government, as defined in ORS 174.111, are directed to assist
3 the task force in the performance of the duties of the task force and, to the extent permitted
4 by laws relating to confidentiality, to furnish information and advice the members of the task
5 force consider necessary to perform their duties.
6 (14) All appointments to the task force made under subsection (2) of this section must
7 be completed no later than 60 days after the effective date of this 2026 Act.
8 (15) The task force shall have its first meeting no later than 90 days after the effective
9 date of this 2026 Act.
10 SECTION 2. Section 1 of this 2026 Act is repealed on December 31, 2027.
11
12 OREGON OCEAN SCIENCE TRUST
13
14 SECTION 3. Pursuant to ORS 196.566, the Oregon Ocean Science Trust may create and
15 maintain an entity that is exempt from federal income tax under section 501(c)(3) of the
16 Internal Revenue Code, as amended, for the purpose of advancing the mission of the trust.
17
18 CAPTIONS AND EFFECTIVE DATE
19
20 SECTION 4. The unit captions used in this 2026 Act are provided only for the convenience
21 of the reader and do not become part of the statutory law of this state or express any leg-
22 islative intent in the enactment of this 2026 Act.
23 SECTION 5. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
24 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.
