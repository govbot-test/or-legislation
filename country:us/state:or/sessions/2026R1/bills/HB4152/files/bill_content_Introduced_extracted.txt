Section 1:
House Bill 4152
Sponsored by Representative WATANABE; Representatives BOWMAN, GOMBERG, ISADORE, NOSSE, SMITH G,
WISE, Senators FREDERICK, GELSER BLOUIN, MEEK (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: The Act lets deployed people get married even if they cannot be there in person if cer-
tain criteria are met. (Flesch Readability Score: 60.6).
Modifies provisions regarding solemnization of marriage to permit deployed service members to
be married remotely.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to remote marriage solemnization of deployed persons.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. (1) As used in this section:
5 (a) “Armed Forces of the United States” includes the regular and reserve components
6 of the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard and Space Force of the United
7 States and the National Guard.
8 (b) “Communication technology” means an electronic device or process that allows two
9 or more individuals who are not in the physical presence of each other to communicate si-
10 multaneously by sight and sound.
11 (c) “Judicial officer” means a judicial officer described in ORS 106.120 (1)(a)(A).
12 (d) “Service member” means a member of the Armed Forces of the United States.
13 (2) If one of the parties to an application for a marriage license is unable to submit the
14 application to the clerk of court in person by reason of active service, the county clerk may
15 not refuse to issue the marriage license solely because the service member is not physically
16 present.
17 (3) Notwithstanding ORS 106.150 (1), if one of the parties to a solemnization of marriage
18 is a service member who is unable to be physically present at the time of solemnization by
19 reason of active service, the marriage may be solemnized without the service member’s
20 physical presence only if:
21 (a) The marriage is solemnized by a judicial officer;
22 (b) The service member appears remotely using communication technology; and
23 (c) All other requirements under ORS 106.150 (1) are met.
24 (4) The judicial officer may waive the fee required under ORS 106.120 (4) for a marriage
25 solemnized as provided in this section.
