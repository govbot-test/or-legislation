SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act lets deployed people get married even if they cannot be there in person if cer-
tain criteria are met. (Flesch Readability Score: 60.6).
Modifies provisions regarding solemnization of marriage to permit deployed service members to
be married remotely.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to remote marriage solemnization of deployed persons.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. (1) As used in this section:
5 (a) “Armed Forces of the United States” includes the regular and reserve components
6 of the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard and Space Force of the United
7 States and the National Guard.
8 (b) “Communication technology” means an electronic device or process that allows two
9 or more individuals who are not in the physical presence of each other to communicate si-
10 multaneously by sight and sound.
11 (c) “Judicial officer” means a judicial officer described in ORS 106.120 (1)(a)(A).
12 (d) “Service member” means a member of the Armed Forces of the United States.
13 (2) If one of the parties to an application for a marriage license is unable to submit the
14 application to the county clerk in person by reason of active service:
15 (a) The county clerk may not refuse to issue the marriage license solely because the
16 service member is not physically present; and
17 (b) The service member may sign the application electronically.
18 (3) Notwithstanding ORS 106.150 (1), if one of the parties to a solemnization of marriage
19 is a service member who is unable to be physically present at the time of solemnization by
20 reason of active service, the marriage may be solemnized without the service member’s
21 physical presence only if:
22 (a) The marriage is solemnized by a judicial officer;
23 (b) The service member appears remotely using communication technology; and
24 (c) All other requirements under ORS 106.150 (1) are met.
25 (4) The judicial officer may waive the fee required under ORS 106.120 (4) for a marriage
26 solemnized as provided in this section.
