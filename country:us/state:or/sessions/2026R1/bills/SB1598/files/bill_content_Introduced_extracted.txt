SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: Updates the preventive health services that must be covered by some health benefit
plans. Gives the Public Health Officer the power to make some health benefit plans provide coverage
for vaccines. Gives the Public Health Officer the power to issue a standing order for a prescription.
Takes effect when the Governor signs it. (Flesch Readability Score: 62.4).
Requires certain health benefit plans to provide coverage for preventive health services in ac-
cordance with federal rules in effect on June 30, 2025, and immunizations recommended by the
Public Health Officer in the future.
Authorizes the Public Health Officer, or designated physician, to issue a standing order for a
prescription to control, prevent, mitigate or treat any infectious or noninfectious disease or other
significant public health concern.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.

11 SECTION 2. (1) As used in this section:
12 (a) “Administer” means the direct application of a drug or device whether by injection,
13 inhalation, ingestion or other means, to the body of a patient by:
14 (A) A practitioner or practitioner’s authorized agent; or
15 (B) The patient at the direction of the practitioner.
16 (b) “Deliver” or “delivery” means the actual, constructive or attempted transfer of a
17 drug or device other than by administration from one person to another, whether or not for
18 a consideration.
19 (c) “Device” means an instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, contrivance, implant,
20 in vitro reagent or other similar or related article, including any component part or acces-
21 sory, that is required under federal or state law to be prescribed by a practitioner and dis-
22 pensed by a pharmacist.
23 (d) “Dispense” means the preparation and delivery of a prescription drug, pursuant to a
24 lawful order of a practitioner, in a suitable container appropriately labeled for subsequent
25 administration to or use by a patient or other individual entitled to receive the prescription
26 drug.
27 (e) “Drug” means:
28 (A) Articles recognized as drugs in the official United States Pharmacopoeia, official
29 National Formulary, official Homeopathic Pharmacopoeia, other drug compendium or any
30 supplement to any of them;
31 (B) Articles intended for use in the diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment or prevention
32 of disease in a human or other animal;
33 (C) Articles, other than food, intended to affect the structure or any function of the body
34 of humans or other animals; and
35 (D) Articles intended for use as a component of any articles specified in subparagraph
36 (A), (B) or (C) of this paragraph.
37 (f) “Practitioner” means a person licensed and operating within the scope of such license
38 to prescribe, dispense or administer drugs or devices in the course of professional practice.
39 (2)(a) The Public Health Officer appointed under ORS 431.045, or a physician licensed
40 under ORS chapter 677 who is employed by the Oregon Health Authority and designated by
41 the Public Health Officer, may issue a standing order to prescribe a drug or device to con-
42 trol, prevent, mitigate or treat any infectious or noninfectious disease or other significant
43 public health concern.
44 (b) In developing a standing order, the Public Health Officer shall rely upon evidence and
45 consider guidance and recommendations from medical and scientific organizations.
