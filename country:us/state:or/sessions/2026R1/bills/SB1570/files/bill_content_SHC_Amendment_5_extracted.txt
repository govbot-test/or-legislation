3 Delete lines 4 through 8 and insert:
4 “SECTION 1. (1) As used in this section:
5 “(a) ‘Hospital’ has the meaning given that term in ORS 442.015.
6 “(b) ‘Individually identifiable health information’ has the meaning
7 given that term in ORS 192.556.
8 “(c) ‘Information concerning a person’s citizenship or immigration
9 status’ has the meaning given that term in ORS 180.805.
10 “(d) ‘Law enforcement authority’ includes a federal, state and local
11 law enforcement authority.
12 “(2) A hospital shall designate which areas of the hospital are not
13 open to the public. Areas designated as not open to the public must
14 include any area:
15 “(a) Where entry or access to the area is controlled; or
16 “(b) That is a patient room.
17 “(3) A hospital may not disclose for the purpose of law enforcement
18 individually identifiable health information or information concerning
19 a person’s citizenship or immigration status unless disclosure is:
20 “(a) Required by state or federal law or an order of a court of
21 competent jurisdiction, including a judicial warrant; or

1 “(b) For the purpose of ensuring continuity of care.
2 “(4) A hospital shall adopt and maintain written policies and pro-
3 cedures that:
4 “(a) Address how the hospital will respond if a law enforcement
5 authority arrives at the hospital; and
6 “(b) Identify for the hospital at least one administrator in a super-
7 visory role who is designated to respond when a law enforcement au-
8 thority arrives at the hospital.
9 “SECTION 2. (1) As used in this section, ‘health care facility’
10 means:
11 “(a) A hospital as defined in ORS 442.015.
12 “(b) A federally qualified health center as defined in 42 U.S.C.
13 1396d(l)(2).
14 “(2) It is an unlawful employment practice for a health care facility
15 to retaliate or take disciplinary action against an employee of the
16 health care facility because the employee has distributed informational
17 or educational materials concerning immigrant rights and available
18 immigration legal services if the materials are published or otherwise
19 made available by a state agency.
20 “(3) An employee who alleges a violation of this section may file a
21 complaint with the Commissioner of the Bureau of Labor and Indus-
22 tries in the manner provided by ORS 659A.820.
23 “(4) Nothing in this section prohibits a collective bargaining agree-
24 ment from establishing terms that are more protective than the re-
25 quirements under this section.
26 “SECTION 3. Section 4 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part
27 of ORS 192.553 to 192.581.
28 “SECTION 4. (1) As used in this section:
29 “(a) ‘Health care facility’ means:
30 “(A) A hospital as defined in ORS 442.015.
