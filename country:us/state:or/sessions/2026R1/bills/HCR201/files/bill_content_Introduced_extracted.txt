Section 1:
House Concurrent Resolution 201
Sponsored by Representatives BOICE, SCHARF, WRIGHT; Representatives BUNCH, DIEHL, EDWARDS, EVANS,
GOMBERG, HARBICK, HELFRICH, LEWIS, MANNIX, MARSH, MCINTIRE, OSBORNE, OWENS,
SKARLATOS, YUNKER, Senators DRAZAN, FREDERICK, GIROD, GOLDEN, SMITH DB (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: Honors an important agency that helps fight fires as it turns 75 years old. (Flesch
Readability Score: 71.7).
Celebrates the 75th anniversary of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association.
1 CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 Whereas the first fire district in Oregon was established due to the efforts of Glenn Leininger,
3 the first president of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association; and
4 Whereas in July of 1932, when Glenn Leininger noticed a small group of firefighters watching
5 a house burn near 82nd Avenue and Sandy Boulevard just outside of Portland, he asked them why
6 they weren’t putting out the fire and they told him that they were prohibited from fighting fires
7 outside city limits; and
8 Whereas the next day, Glenn Leininger went to a meeting of the Parkrose Lions Club and de-
9 scribed what he had experienced near 82nd and Sandy; and
10 Whereas Frank Severs, one of the members of the Parkrose Lions Club who heard Leininger’s
11 story, was an attorney and volunteered to develop a petition and legislation for the upcoming ses-
12 sion of the Legislative Assembly; and
13 Whereas in 1933, Glenn Leininger and Frank Severs went to Salem, and their work with the
14 Thirty-seventh Legislative Assembly led to the passage of a bill allowing for the creation of fire
15 districts in Oregon; and
16 Whereas on November 15, 1933, the Oregon Commissioner of Insurance designated the Parkrose
17 neighborhood as Oregon Rural Fire Protection District #1; and
18 Whereas in 1950, the first attempt to organize a statewide association of Oregon’s approximately
19 119 fire districts began with a meeting at the Keizer Fire Hall; and
20 Whereas Glenn Leininger and Frank Severs attended the meeting that generated proposed
21 ground rules and organizational rules for the association, and those rules were presented at the first
22 organizational meeting of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association on June 2, 1950, in the
23 chambers of Salem City Council; and
24 Whereas after adopting rules and membership requirements, the group voted to invite the
25 Oregon Fire Chiefs Association to hold a joint conference, and at that conference Glenn Leininger
26 was elected as the first President of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association, along with of-
27 ficers Walter A. Morgan and Harold Rosebrough; and
28 Whereas in 1988, Marion County Fire Chief Ted Miller located and facilitated the purchase of
29 a building located on Court Street in Salem, and over the years, that building, which would be

1 named the Ted Miller Oregon Fire Service Center, has housed the Oregon Fire Chiefs Association,
2 the Oregon Volunteer Firefighters Association, the Oregon Fire Service Museum, Memorial and
3 Learning Center, the Oregon Fire Marshals Association and the Oregon Fire Service Office Admin-
4 istrators organization; and
5 Whereas the Board of Directors of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association offers the use
6 of the Ted Miller Fire Service Center to all fire service agencies; and
7 Whereas the board and staff of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association believe in coop-
8 eration and collaboration with all fire service agencies, including the Oregon Fire Chiefs Associ-
9 ation, the Oregon Volunteer Firefighters Association, the Oregon Fire Service Museum, Memorial
10 and Learning Center, the Oregon Fire Marshals Association, the Oregon Fire Service Office Ad-
11 ministrators organization, the Oregon State Firefighters Council, the Special Districts Association
12 of Oregon, the Federation of Fire Chaplains, Oregon Chapter and the Department of the State Fire
13 Marshal; and
14 Whereas the mission of the Oregon Fire District Directors Association is to promote education,
15 coordination and legislation that advances public safety to the betterment of all Oregonians; and
16 Whereas the Oregon Fire District Directors Association sponsors the Fire Service Conference,
17 which offers training opportunities for all fire service personnel; and
18 Whereas in November 2025, the Oregon Fire District Directors Association celebrated its 75th
19 Fire Service Conference at Seaside, Oregon; now, therefore,
20 Be It Resolved by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Oregon:
21 That we, the members of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly, recognize and honor the Oregon
22 Fire District Directors Association for more than 75 years of promoting safety; and be it further
23 Resolved, That we celebrate the significant contributions of the Oregon Fire District Directors
24 Association to the State of Oregon and we encourage its continued collaboration with agencies
25 throughout this state in protecting the lives and property of all Oregonians.
