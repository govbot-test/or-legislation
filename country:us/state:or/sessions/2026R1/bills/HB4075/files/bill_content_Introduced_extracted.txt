83rd OREGON LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY--2026 Regular Session
House Bill 4075
Sponsored by Representative WRIGHT, Senator SMITH DB, Representative JAVADI; Representatives HELFRICH,
MANNIX, OSBORNE, SCHARF, Senator WEBER (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: This Act lets the State Treasurer loan unclaimed moneys to rural hospitals. (Flesch
Readability Score: 60.7).
Authorizes the State Treasurer to loan up to 20 percent of the Unclaimed Property and Estates
Fund to rural hospitals for financial stabilization.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to hospital finance; creating new provisions; amending ORS 98.389 and 293.701; and de-
3 claring an emergency.
4 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
5 SECTION 1. (1) The State Treasurer may use up to 20 percent of the Unclaimed Property
6 and Estates Fund established under ORS 98.389 to issue rural hospital stabilization loans
7 under this section.
8 (2) Rural hospital stabilization loans are available only to hospitals that:
9 (a) Are rural hospitals, as defined in ORS 442.470;
10 (b) Are owned and operated by a publicly elected board; and
11 (c) Receive Medicare reimbursement on the basis of diagnostic related groups.
12 (3) Rural hospital stabilization loans may only be used to address cash shortages or to
13 refinance existing debt and may not be used to expand services, facilities or operations or
14 to make capital improvements.
15 (4) To be eligible for a rural hospital stabilization loan, a hospital must include in its
16 application:
17 (a) A stabilization plan that includes financial forecasts and near-term plans showing
18 reductions in expenses or increases to revenue, including through anticipated grants or other
19 assistance that is not yet realized; and
20 (b) A statement by the Director of the Oregon Health Authority approving the plan.
21 (5) Interest charged on a rural hospital stabilization loan may not be less than the ap-
22 plicable federal funds rate at the time the loan was issued.
23 (6) In determining whether to issue a rural hospital stabilization loan, the State Treas-
24 urer may consult with the Oregon Health Authority and may consider any factors, including:
25 (a) The repayment feasibility over the term of the loan;
26 (b) The expected improvements to the hospital’s operations if the loan is provided; and
27 (c) The expected public benefits, including benefits to economic activity and to access to
28 health care, if the loan is provided.
