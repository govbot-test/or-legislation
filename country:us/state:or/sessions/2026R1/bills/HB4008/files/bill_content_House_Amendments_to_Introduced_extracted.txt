Section 1:
HOUSE AMENDMENTS TO
HOUSE BILL 4008
By COMMITTEE ON TRANSPORTATION
February 18
1 On page 1 of the printed bill, delete lines 4 through 28.
2 On page 2, delete lines 1 through 44 and insert:
3 “SECTION 1. (1) The Transit Funding Task Force is established.
4 “(2) The task force consists of 22 members appointed as follows:
5 “(a) From among members of the Senate:
6 “(A) The President of the Senate shall appoint one nonvoting member; and
7 “(B) The Senate Minority Leader shall appoint one nonvoting member.
8 “(b) From among members of the House of Representatives:
9 “(A) The Speaker of the House of Representatives shall appoint one nonvoting member;
10 and
11 “(B) The House Minority Leader shall appoint one nonvoting member.
12 “(c) Jointly, the President of the Senate and Speaker of the House of Representatives
13 shall appoint the following nonlegislative members:
14 “(A) One member who represents the City of Portland;
15 “(B) One member who represents the Amalgamated Transit Union;
16 “(C) One member who represents a workers’ union;
17 “(D) One member who represents a mass transit provider;
18 “(E) One member who represents a business association representing the Portland met-
19 ropolitan area;
20 “(F) One member who represents a business association representing businesses outside
21 the Portland metropolitan area;
22 “(G) One member who represents a tribal transit provider;
23 “(H) One member who represents a transit provider that provides services in a city with
24 a population of less than 100,000;
25 “(I) One member who represents a rural transit provider;
26 “(J) One member who represents a transit provider that primarily serves people with
27 disabilities and seniors;
28 “(K) One member who represents Metro, as defined in ORS 197.015;
29 “(L) One member who represents city governments in this state;
30 “(M) One member who represents county governments in this state;
31 “(N) One member who represents an organization that advocates for transportation
32 safety;
33 “(O) One member who represents transit riders who utilize a low-income transit pass
34 program;
35 “(P) One member who represents an employer with over 100 employees;
LC 257/HB 4008-5
1 “(Q) One member who represents an employer with 100 employees or fewer; and
2 “(R) One member who serves as a nonvoting representative of the Department of
3 Transportation.
4 “(3) The task force shall:
5 “(a) Determine the level of funding needed to maintain adequate transit service statewide
6 that is reliable, safe and accessible and allows for population growth over time; and
7 “(b) Explore funding mechanisms to achieve the funding needs determined in paragraph
8 (a) of this subsection.
9 “(4) A majority of the voting members of the task force constitutes a quorum for the
10 transaction of business.
11 “(5) Official action by the task force requires the approval of a majority of the voting
12 members of the task force.
13 “(6) The President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall
14 select one member of the task force to serve as chairperson and another to serve as vice
15 chairperson, with the duties and powers necessary for the performance of the functions of
16 the offices as the President and the Speaker determine.
17 “(7) If there is a vacancy for any cause, the appointing authority shall make an appoint-
18 ment to become immediately effective.
19 “(8) The task force shall meet at times and places specified by the call of the chairperson
20 or of a majority of the voting members of the task force.
21 “(9) The task force may adopt rules necessary for the operation of the task force.
22 “(10) The task force shall submit a report in the manner provided by ORS 192.245, and
23 may include recommendations for legislation, to an interim committee of the Legislative
24 Assembly related to transportation no later than December 1, 2026. The report must include
25 recommended revenue mechanisms including the following information about each recom-
26 mended mechanism:
27 “(a) The amount of revenue the mechanism is projected to raise;
28 “(b) Who would pay; and
29 “(c) The likelihood of the revenue source being a reliable source of revenue in the future.
30 “(11) The Legislative Revenue Office shall provide staff support to the task force.
31 “(12) Members of the Legislative Assembly appointed to the task force are nonvoting
32 members of the task force and may act in an advisory capacity only.
33 “(13) Members of the task force who are not members of the Legislative Assembly serve
34 as volunteers on the task force and, unless they are qualified members, as defined in ORS
35 292.495, are not entitled to compensation or reimbursement for expenses.
36 “(14) All agencies of state government, as defined in ORS 174.111, are directed to assist
37 the task force in the performance of the duties of the task force and, to the extent permitted
38 by laws relating to confidentiality, to furnish information and advice the members of the task
39 force consider necessary to perform their duties.”.
