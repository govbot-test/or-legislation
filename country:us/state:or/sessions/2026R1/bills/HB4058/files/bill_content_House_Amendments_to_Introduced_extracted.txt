83rd OREGON LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY--2026 Regular Session
HOUSE AMENDMENTS TO
HOUSE BILL 4058
By COMMITTEE ON EARLY CHILDHOOD AND HUMAN SERVICES
February 16
1 Delete lines 4 through 16 of the printed bill and insert:
2 “SECTION 1. (1) Notwithstanding ORS 418.321 or 418.322, if there is reason to know, as
3 described in ORS 419B.636, that a child is an Indian child, the Department of Human Services
4 may place the child in an out-of-state institution meeting the criteria described in ORS
5 419B.654 (1)(d)(B)(iv) without requiring the institution to be licensed by or under contract
6 with the department or to be a qualified residential treatment program if:
7 “(a) The placement complies with the placement preferences under ORS 419B.654; and
8 “(b) The department provides at least 10 days’ notice to the court and the parties of the
9 proposed placement or, if the delay caused by providing this notice would likely result in
10 serious physical or emotional harm to the child, endanger the child’s life or cause loss of
11 placement availability, the department shall provide the notice to the court and the parties
12 as soon as practicable and in no event later than one business day after the child leaves this
13 state.
14 “(2) ORS 418.321 (1) to (4) or (6) does not apply to the department’s placement of a child
15 in an out-of-state child-caring agency if:
16 “(a) The placement is for the purpose of placing the child or ward in a relative foster
17 home or preadoptive family placement that has been selected and approved by the depart-
18 ment;
19 “(b) The department retains the sole authority to approve the foster care or preadoptive
20 placement of the child;
21 “(c) The child is not placed in a congregate care residential setting, as defined in ORS
22 418.322; and
23 “(d) The placement complies with the requirements of the Interstate Compact on the
24 Placement of Children.
25 “(3) As used in this section:
26 “(a) ‘Child’ has the meaning given that term in ORS 418.205.
27 “(b) ‘Indian child’ has the meaning given that term in ORS 419B.603.
28 “(c) ‘Out-of-state child-caring agency’ means a provider of children’s care or services in
29 a state other than Oregon that would be required to be licensed, certified or otherwise au-
30 thorized by the department under ORS 418.240 if the provider provided the care or services
31 in this state.
32 “(d) ‘Qualified residential treatment program’ means a program described in ORS
33 418.323.”.
