4 “SECTION 1. ORS 329A.500 is amended to read:
5 “329A.500. (1) As used in this section, ‘family’ means any individual who
6 is responsible for the care, control and supervision of a child.
7 “(2) The Early Learning Council shall adopt rules for the operation of the
8 Employment Related Day Care subsidy program and for other subsidy pro-
9 grams administered by the Department of Early Learning and Care.
10 “(3) The rules adopted under this section must support equitable access
11 to a supply of diverse subsidized care facilities that meet the needs of fami-
12 lies, as those needs are defined by the council by rule, including:
13 “(a) Cultural diversity;
14 “(b) Linguistic diversity;
15 “(c) Racial and ethnic diversity; and
16 “(d) Diversity of subsidized care facility types.
17 “(4) The council shall adopt rules establishing that:
18 “(a) Each of the following subparagraphs constitutes an independent basis
19 of eligibility for a child to participate in the Employment Related Day Care
20 subsidy program:
21 “(A) The household income of a child’s family;

1 “(B) The child’s family’s employment, search for employment or enroll-
2 ment in an education or training program;
3 “(C) The child’s or child’s family’s use of, or risk of needing to use, pro-
4 tective services, including, but not limited to, child welfare services, child
5 abuse prevention services, family reunification services, foster care services
6 or subsidized guardianship services;
7 “(D) The child’s or child’s family’s use of, or risk of needing to use,
8 homelessness services, teen parent services or domestic violence services; or
9 “(E) Any other criteria established by the council.
10 “(b) A child must be able to receive care that:
11 “(A) Meets the child’s developmental, disability and neurodiversity needs;
12 and
13 “(B) Enables the child’s family to complete activities that relate to family
14 well-being, which may include the family’s work hours, education hours,
15 commute time, study time and other activities that support family well-being.
16 “(c) Payment to subsidized care facilities must be:
17 “(A) Based on enrollment instead of attendance.
18 “(B) Made by the department each month by a date specified by the Early
19 Learning Council by rule or, if paid on a date later than the date specified
20 by rule, paid in an amount that includes an additional nine percent of the
21 amount due unless the additional amount is not required to be paid under
22 exceptional circumstances, as identified by rule.
23 “(d) A child’s family may qualify for an incentive if the family voluntarily
24 chooses a subsidized care facility that participates in the quality recognition
25 and improvement system established under ORS 329A.261. A fair represen-
26 tation of the recipients who qualify for incentives must be families with
27 children who are from underserved racial, ethnic or minority populations. In
28 addition, subsidized care facilities that participate in the quality recognition
29 and improvement system may receive financial incentives under the subsidy
30 programs in accordance with criteria established by rule.
