Section 1:
A-Engrossed
Senate Bill 1535
Ordered by the Senate February 16
Including Senate Amendments dated February 16
Sponsored by Senator ANDERSON; Senator SMITH DB (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act allows ELC to change some standards for who can use a day care subsidy
program. The Act tells DELC to create a work group to study and report to the Senate and
the House on liability insurance issues for child care providers and on how to increase access
and reduce costs for those providers. The Act sunsets the work group in 2029. The Act takes
effect 91 days after session ends. (Flesch Readability Score: 67.7).
[Digest: The Act tells DELC to study child care and submit a report to the legislature. (Flesch
Readability Score: 73.1).]
[Requires the Department of Early Learning and Care to study child care. Directs the department
to submit findings to the interim committees of the Legislative Assembly related to child care no later
than September 15, 2027.]
[Sunsets on January 2, 2028.]
Allows the Early Learning Council to modify the priority given to families receiving
temporary assistance for needy families for participation in the Employment Related Day
Care subsidy program.
Directs the Department of Early Learning and Care to convene a work group to study
the cost and availability of liability insurance and other liability issues for child care pro-
viders. Identifies work group participants. Directs the work group to report to the Legisla-
tive Assembly on options to increase child care provider access to liability insurance, to
reduce the financial burden of liability insurance on child care providers and to use other
strategies to address liability issues for child care providers. Requires the work group to
submit the report to the Legislative Assembly by November 1, 2027. Sunsets the work group
on January 2, 2029.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.

5 SECTION 1. ORS 329A.500 is amended to read:
6 329A.500. (1) As used in this section, “family” means any individual who is responsible for the
7 care, control and supervision of a child.
8 (2) The Early Learning Council shall adopt rules for the operation of the Employment Related
9 Day Care subsidy program and for other subsidy programs administered by the Department of Early
10 Learning and Care.
11 (3) The rules adopted under this section must support equitable access to a supply of diverse
12 subsidized care facilities that meet the needs of families, as those needs are defined by the council
13 by rule, including:
14 (a) Cultural diversity;
15 (b) Linguistic diversity;
16 (c) Racial and ethnic diversity; and

1 (d) Diversity of subsidized care facility types.
2 (4) The council shall adopt rules establishing that:
3 (a) Each of the following subparagraphs constitutes an independent basis of eligibility for a child
4 to participate in the Employment Related Day Care subsidy program:
5 (A) The household income of a child’s family;
6 (B) The child’s family’s employment, search for employment or enrollment in an education or
7 training program;
8 (C) The child’s or child’s family’s use of, or risk of needing to use, protective services, including,
9 but not limited to, child welfare services, child abuse prevention services, family reunification ser-
10 vices, foster care services or subsidized guardianship services;
11 (D) The child’s or child’s family’s use of, or risk of needing to use, homelessness services, teen
12 parent services or domestic violence services; or
13 (E) Any other criteria established by the council.
14 (b) A child must be able to receive care that:
15 (A) Meets the child’s developmental, disability and neurodiversity needs; and
16 (B) Enables the child’s family to complete activities that relate to family well-being, which may
17 include the family’s work hours, education hours, commute time, study time and other activities that
18 support family well-being.
19 (c) Payment to subsidized care facilities must be:
20 (A) Based on enrollment instead of attendance.
21 (B) Made by the department each month by a date specified by the Early Learning Council by
22 rule or, if paid on a date later than the date specified by rule, paid in an amount that includes an
23 additional nine percent of the amount due unless the additional amount is not required to be paid
24 under exceptional circumstances, as identified by rule.
25 (d) A child’s family may qualify for an incentive if the family voluntarily chooses a subsidized
26 care facility that participates in the quality recognition and improvement system established under
27 ORS 329A.261. A fair representation of the recipients who qualify for incentives must be families
28 with children who are from underserved racial, ethnic or minority populations. In addition, subsi-
29 dized care facilities that participate in the quality recognition and improvement system may receive
30 financial incentives under the subsidy programs in accordance with criteria established by rule.
31 (e) A child care facility is not eligible to be a subsidized care facility and to receive a re-
32 imbursement under the subsidy program unless each subject individual described in ORS 329A.030
33 (11)(d) who operates, resides in or may have unsupervised contact with children at the subsidized
34 care facility that provides or will provide subsidized care to the person’s child is enrolled in the
35 Central Background Registry under ORS 329A.030.
36 (f) A subsidized care facility may be eligible to receive a higher rate of reimbursement or other
37 financial incentives for:
38 (A) Participating in quality improvement measures;
39 (B) Providing culturally or linguistically specific or appropriate care;
40 (C) Providing evening, overnight or weekend care;
41 (D) Providing care to children with a diagnosed disability;
42 (E) Providing infant or toddler care;
43 (F) Providing care to a population that has been identified as historically having an inadequate
44 child care facility supply; or
45 (G) Providing any other specialized care that justifies a higher rate of reimbursement.

1 (5) Taking into account the availability of funds, the rules adopted under this section:
2 (a) Must establish a sliding scale for copayment, with the requirement that a copayment may
3 not exceed seven percent of the household income of the child’s family.
4 (b) Must provide that eligibility to participate in the Employment Related Day Care subsidy
5 program:
6 (A) May not be based on the citizenship or legal status of a child or a child’s family; and
7 (B) Shall, for a child who met the initial eligibility requirements prescribed under subsection (4)
8 of this section, continue for a minimum of 12 months from the date of initial eligibility unless the
9 child’s family leaves this state or requests a termination of benefits or for any other reason identi-
10 fied by the council. Rules adopted under this subparagraph [shall] may give priority to families re-
11 ceiving temporary assistance under the temporary assistance for needy families program described
12 in ORS 412.006.
13 (c) May provide that a determination of eligibility to participate in the Employment Related Day
14 Care subsidy program consider the availability of family to attend to the child, regardless of the
15 family’s physical presence.
16 (6) In developing rules under this section, the council shall consider policies for increasing the
17 stability and continuity of a child’s access to a family’s preferred child care facility.
18 (7) Rules adopted by the council under this section establish minimum requirements pertaining
19 to the Employment Related Day Care subsidy program and may not be construed to preempt, limit
20 or otherwise diminish the applicability of any policy, standard or collective bargaining agreement
21 that provides for an increased subsidy or a subsidized care facility reimbursement amount under
22 state or federal law.
23 (8)(a) The council shall work to meet federal recommendations for income eligibility and market
24 access in regard to the Employment Related Day Care subsidy program administered by the council.
25 (b) Notwithstanding any provision of this section or any rule adopted by the council pursuant
26 to this section, the laws and regulations applicable to any federal funds shall govern when any as-
27 pect of child care is funded by federal funds.
28 SECTION 2. (1) The Department of Early Learning and Care shall convene a work group
29 to study the cost and availability of liability insurance and other liability issues for child care
30 providers certified under ORS 329A.280, registered under ORS 329A.330 or recorded under
31 ORS 329A.255. The work group may also study impacts to programs or entities that would
32 typically be exempt from Department of Early Learning and Care regulation. The objective
33 of the work group is to develop strategies for lowering insurance costs for these providers
34 in this state.
35 (2) The work group shall consist of members selected by the Department of Early
36 Learning and Care, in consultation with the Department of Consumer and Business Services.
37 (3) When making selections for the work group under subsection (2) of this section, the
38 Department of Early Learning and Care shall, to the extent practicable, select the following
39 as members:
40 (a) Representatives from certified, registered, recorded and exempt child care providers
41 located in different regions of this state.
42 (b) Representatives from the insurance industry with experience in liability insurance
43 offered to child care facilities certified under ORS 329A.280, registered under ORS 329A.330
44 or recorded under ORS 329A.255, or to other types of exempt child care that are not subject
45 to Department of Early Learning and Care regulation.
[3]
A-Eng. SB 1535
1 (c) Representatives from the Department of Consumer and Business Services with ex-
2 pertise in liability insurance regulation.
3 (d) Representatives from the Department of Early Learning and Care with expertise in
4 licensing and insurance requirements for providers of early learning services and care.
5 (e) Representatives from the Oregon Department of Administrative Services with exper-
6 tise in risk management, including expertise in establishing contractor and grantee insur-
7 ance requirements.
8 (f) Any other representatives that the Department of Early Learning and Care deter-
9 mines are necessary to fulfill the purposes of this section.
10 (4) The Department of Early Learning and Care may select up to five additional members
11 provided that such members have experience relevant to the objective of the work group as
12 described in subsection (1) of this section.
13 (5) Members of the work group must be individuals who reflect the geographic, racial,
14 ethnic and gender diversity of this state.
15 (6) The work group shall review information and research from within Oregon and na-
16 tionally concerning the impact of liability insurance and regulations pertaining to insurance
17 requirements applicable to providers of child care and child care facilities as defined in ORS
18 329A.250, including certain types of care that are exempt from Department of Early Learning
19 and Care regulation.
20 (7) The work group shall prepare a report that summarizes the findings of the review
21 required under subsection (6) of this section. The report must:
22 (a) Include policy options to increase child care provider access to liability insurance and
23 to reduce the financial burden of liability insurance on providers of child care; and
24 (b) Explore the feasibility of using liability immunity, liability damage caps or other
25 strategies to address liability issues that affect the availability and affordability of liability
26 insurance for providers of early learning services and care.
27 (8) Not later than November 1, 2027, the work group shall submit the report required by
28 this section, in the manner provided under ORS 192.245, to the interim committees of the
29 Legislative Assembly related to early child care. The work group may include in the report
30 submitted under this subsection recommendations for legislation.
31 SECTION 3. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is repealed on January 2, 2029.
32 SECTION 4. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
33 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.
