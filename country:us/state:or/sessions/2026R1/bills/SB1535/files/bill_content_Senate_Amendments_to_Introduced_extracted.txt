4 “SECTION 1. ORS 329A.500 is amended to read:
5 “329A.500. (1) As used in this section, ‘family’ means any individual who is responsible for the
6 care, control and supervision of a child.
7 “(2) The Early Learning Council shall adopt rules for the operation of the Employment Related
8 Day Care subsidy program and for other subsidy programs administered by the Department of Early
9 Learning and Care.
10 “(3) The rules adopted under this section must support equitable access to a supply of diverse
11 subsidized care facilities that meet the needs of families, as those needs are defined by the council
12 by rule, including:
13 “(a) Cultural diversity;
14 “(b) Linguistic diversity;
15 “(c) Racial and ethnic diversity; and
16 “(d) Diversity of subsidized care facility types.
17 “(4) The council shall adopt rules establishing that:
18 “(a) Each of the following subparagraphs constitutes an independent basis of eligibility for a
19 child to participate in the Employment Related Day Care subsidy program:
20 “(A) The household income of a child’s family;
21 “(B) The child’s family’s employment, search for employment or enrollment in an education or
22 training program;
23 “(C) The child’s or child’s family’s use of, or risk of needing to use, protective services, includ-
24 ing, but not limited to, child welfare services, child abuse prevention services, family reunification
25 services, foster care services or subsidized guardianship services;
26 “(D) The child’s or child’s family’s use of, or risk of needing to use, homelessness services, teen
27 parent services or domestic violence services; or
28 “(E) Any other criteria established by the council.
29 “(b) A child must be able to receive care that:
30 “(A) Meets the child’s developmental, disability and neurodiversity needs; and
31 “(B) Enables the child’s family to complete activities that relate to family well-being, which may
32 include the family’s work hours, education hours, commute time, study time and other activities that
33 support family well-being.
34 “(c) Payment to subsidized care facilities must be:
35 “(A) Based on enrollment instead of attendance.

8 “SECTION 2. (1) The Department of Early Learning and Care shall convene a work group
9 to study the cost and availability of liability insurance and other liability issues for child care
10 providers certified under ORS 329A.280, registered under ORS 329A.330 or recorded under
11 ORS 329A.255. The work group may also study impacts to programs or entities that would
12 typically be exempt from Department of Early Learning and Care regulation. The objective
13 of the work group is to develop strategies for lowering insurance costs for these providers
14 in this state.
15 “(2) The work group shall consist of members selected by the Department of Early
16 Learning and Care, in consultation with the Department of Consumer and Business Services.
17 “(3) When making selections for the work group under subsection (2) of this section, the
18 Department of Early Learning and Care shall, to the extent practicable, select the following
19 as members:
20 “(a) Representatives from certified, registered, recorded and exempt child care providers
21 located in different regions of this state.
22 “(b) Representatives from the insurance industry with experience in liability insurance
23 offered to child care facilities certified under ORS 329A.280, registered under ORS 329A.330
24 or recorded under ORS 329A.255, or to other types of exempt child care that are not subject
25 to Department of Early Learning and Care regulation.
26 “(c) Representatives from the Department of Consumer and Business Services with ex-
27 pertise in liability insurance regulation.
28 “(d) Representatives from the Department of Early Learning and Care with expertise in
29 licensing and insurance requirements for providers of early learning services and care.
30 “(e) Representatives from the Oregon Department of Administrative Services with ex-
31 pertise in risk management, including expertise in establishing contractor and grantee in-
32 surance requirements.
33 “(f) Any other representatives that the Department of Early Learning and Care deter-
34 mines are necessary to fulfill the purposes of this section.
35 “(4) The Department of Early Learning and Care may select up to five additional mem-
36 bers provided that such members have experience relevant to the objective of the work group
37 as described in subsection (1) of this section.
38 “(5) Members of the work group must be individuals who reflect the geographic, racial,
39 ethnic and gender diversity of this state.
40 “(6) The work group shall review information and research from within Oregon and na-
41 tionally concerning the impact of liability insurance and regulations pertaining to insurance
42 requirements applicable to providers of child care and child care facilities as defined in ORS
43 329A.250, including certain types of care that are exempt from Department of Early Learning
44 and Care regulation.
45 “(7) The work group shall prepare a report that summarizes the findings of the review
