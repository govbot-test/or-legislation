SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act says a person might be allowed to do certain jobs with animals, like
checking if cows are pregnant, even if they are not veterinarians. Allows the OSVMEB to
adopt rules to explain when this is okay. (Flesch Readability Score: 62.8).
[Digest: The Act says a person might be allowed to do certain jobs with animals, like checking if
cows are pregnant, even if they are not veterinarians. Allows the ODA to adopt rules to explain when
this is okay. (Flesch Readability Score: 65.1).]
Allows an individual to verify whether cattle are pregnant without holding a valid license issued
by the Oregon State Veterinary Medical Examining Board under certain circumstances. Permits the
[State Department of Agriculture] board to adopt rules.
Sunsets on January 1, 2031.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to providing animal health care; creating new provisions; and amending ORS 686.040.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. ORS 686.040 is amended to read:
5 686.040. (1) ORS 686.020 (1)(a) does not apply to the following:
6 (a) Commissioned veterinary officers of the United States Army, or those in the employ of other
7 United States Government agencies while engaged in their official capacity, unless they enter into
8 a private practice.
9 (b) An individual who has received training to verify whether cattle are pregnant and is
10 performing pregnancy verification services on cattle within the scope of the individual’s
11 training.
12 (2) Nothing in ORS 167.322 or 686.020 (1)(a) shall be so construed as to prevent any person or
13 the agent or employee of the person from practicing veterinary medicine and surgery or dentistry
14 in a humane manner on any animal belonging to the person, agent or employee or for gratuitous
15 services or from dehorning and vaccinating cattle for the person, agent or employee.
16 (3) Nothing in ORS 686.020 (1)(a) shall be so construed as to prevent the selling of veterinary
17 remedies and instruments by a licensed pharmacist at the regular place of business of the licensed
18 pharmacist.
19 (4) A practitioner of allied health methods may practice that method on animals without vio-
20 lating ORS 686.020 (1)(a), as long as the practice is in conformance with laws and rules governing
21 the practitioner’s practice and the practice is upon referral from a licensed veterinarian for treat-
22 ment or therapy specified by the veterinarian.
NOTE: Matter in boldfaced type in an amended section is new; matter [italic and bracketed] is existing law to be omitted.
