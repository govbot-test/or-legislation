83rd OREGON LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY--2026 Regular Session
A-Engrossed
Senate Bill 1599
Ordered by the Senate February 13
Including Senate Amendments dated February 13
Sponsored by Senator WAGNER, Representative FAHEY
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act says the vote on the transportation ballot measure will be in May. (Flesch
Readability Score: 77.8).
Moves the election date for the parts of chapter 1, Oregon Laws 2025 (special session), referred
to the people by Referendum Petition 2026-302, to the primary election held on May 19, 2026.
Sets the procedure for the statewide special election on Referendum Petition 2026-302.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to a special election date for Referendum Petition 2026-302; and declaring an emergency.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. The required number of signatures of Referendum Petition 2026-302 having
5 been verified by the Secretary of State under ORS 250.105, the parts of chapter 1, Oregon
6 Laws 2025 (special session), that are referred by Referendum Petition 2026-302 to the people
7 under Article IV, section 1, of the Oregon Constitution, shall be submitted to the people for
8 their approval or rejection at a special election held throughout this state on the same date
9 as the primary election for 2026 set forth under ORS 254.056 (2).
10 SECTION 2. Except as otherwise provided in sections 1 to 6 of this 2026 Act, ORS chap-
11 ters 250, 251 and 254 apply to the special election held on the measure described in section
12 1 of this 2026 Act.
13 SECTION 3. ORS 250.085 applies to the ballot title prepared for the special election held
14 on the measure described in section 1 of this 2026 Act, except that, if the Supreme Court
15 review process has not been completed by March 12, 2026, the latest ballot title certified by
16 the Attorney General shall be printed in the voters’ pamphlet and on the ballot, as provided
17 in ORS 250.067 (3).
18 SECTION 4. (1) Notwithstanding ORS 251.205, 251.215, 251.225, 251.230 and 251.235, the
19 explanatory statement to be printed in the voters’ pamphlet for the measure described in
20 section 1 of this 2026 Act shall be prepared by a joint legislative committee created for this
21 purpose. The explanatory statement shall be filed with the Secretary of State not later than
22 March 12, 2026.
23 (2) Notwithstanding ORS 250.125, 250.127 and 250.131, the financial estimate to be printed
24 in the voters’ pamphlet for the measure described in section 1 of this 2026 Act shall be pre-
25 pared by a joint legislative committee created for this purpose. The financial estimate shall
26 be filed with the Secretary of State not later than March 12, 2026.
