3 “SECTION 1. ORS 164.125 is amended to read:
4 “164.125. (1) A person commits the crime of theft of services if:
5 “(a) With intent to avoid full or partial payment therefor, the person obtains services that are
6 available only for compensation, by force, threat, deception or other means to avoid full or partial
7 payment for the services; or
8 “(b) Having control over the disposition of labor or of business, commercial or industrial
9 equipment or facilities of another, the person uses or diverts to the use of the person or a third
10 person such labor, equipment or facilities with intent to derive for the person or the third person
11 a commercial benefit to which the person or the third person is not entitled.
12 “(2) As used in this section, ‘services’ includes, but is not limited to, labor, professional services,
13 toll facilities, transportation, communications service, entertainment, the supplying of food, lodging
14 or other accommodations in hotels, restaurants or elsewhere, the supplying of equipment for use,
15 and the supplying of commodities of a public utility nature such as gas, electricity, steam and water.
16 ‘Communication service’ includes, but is not limited to, use of telephone, computer and cable tele-
17 vision systems.
18 “(3) Absconding without payment or offer to pay for hotel, restaurant or other services for
19 which compensation is customarily paid immediately upon the receiving of them is prima facie evi-
20 dence that the services were obtained with intent to avoid payment therefor. Obtaining the use of
21 any communication system the use of which is available only for compensation, including but not
22 limited to telephone, computer and cable television systems, or obtaining the use of any services of
23 a public utility nature, without payment or offer to pay for such use is prima facie evidence that the
24 obtaining of the use of such system or the use of such services was gained with intent to avoid
25 payment therefor.
26 “(4) The value of single theft transactions may be added together if the thefts were committed:
27 “(a) Against multiple victims by a similar means within a 30-day period; or
28 “(b) Against the same victim, or two or more persons who are joint owners, within a 180-day
29 period.
30 “(5) Theft of services is:
31 “(a) A Class C misdemeanor if the aggregate total value of services that are the subject of the
32 theft is less than $100;
33 “(b) A Class A misdemeanor if the aggregate total value of services that are the subject of the
34 theft is $100 or more and less than $1,000;
35 “(c) A Class C felony if the aggregate total value of services that are the subject of the theft
