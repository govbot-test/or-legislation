SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act says that data about a party’s or a witness’s immigration status may
not be used in a civil case. The Act makes some exceptions. The Act makes it unlawful for
employers to punish employees for taking certain actions after a lawful change in their work
authorization documents. The Act makes changes to the definition of “profiling.” The Act
says that state and local law enforcement and the DOC can work with federal immigration
officers with respect to a person who is found guilty of a certain crime for which a victim
was named in the charges. The Act becomes law 91 days after sine die. (Flesch Readability
Score: 60.6).
[Digest: The Act says that data about a party’s or a witness’s immigration status may not be used

Provides that evidence of a party’s or a witness’s immigration status is not admissible as evi-
dence in a civil proceeding. Provides exceptions.
Makes it unlawful for employers to discriminate, retaliate or take other adverse action against
an employee because the employee updates, or attempts to update, the employee’s personal infor-
mation based on a lawful change to the employee’s employment authorization documentation. Clari-
fies what actions constitute an unlawful practice.
For purposes of law enforcement profiling requirements, modifies the definition of “profiling” to
include immigration status.
Permits state and local law enforcement agencies, including the Department of Cor-
rections, to coordinate with any federal immigration authority to carry out actions con-
cerning persons convicted of certain felony crimes.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.

6 ADMISSIBILITY OF EVIDENCE IN CIVIL CASES
7
8 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 40.
9 SECTION 2. (1) Except as otherwise allowed in this section, evidence of a party’s or a
10 witness’s immigration status is not admissible in a civil proceeding unless the party’s or
11 witness’s immigration status is an essential fact to prove an element of a party’s cause of
12 action or essential to establish a party’s claim for relief.

32 SECTION 3. Section 4 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 659A.
33 SECTION 4. (1) It is an unlawful practice for an employer to discharge an employee or
34 in any manner discriminate, retaliate or otherwise take adverse action against an employee
35 because the employee updates or attempts to update the employee’s personal information
36 based on a lawful change in the employee’s federal employment authorization documentation.
37 (2) It is not an unlawful practice under this section for an employer to take actions
38 necessary to comply with federal employment authorization verification requirements.
39 (3) An employer does not engage in an unlawful practice under this section solely because
40 a third-party benefit administrator independently takes adverse action in response to
41 changes in the employee’s personal information or federal employment authorization.
