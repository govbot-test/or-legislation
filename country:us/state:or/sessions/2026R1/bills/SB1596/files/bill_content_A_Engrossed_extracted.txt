Section 1:
A-Engrossed
Senate Bill 1596
Ordered by the Senate February 13
Including Senate Amendments dated February 13
Sponsored by Senators FREDERICK, MANNING JR; Senators CAMPOS, GOLDEN, JAMA, PROZANSKI, WEBER,
Representatives ANDERSEN, GAMBA, HELM, ISADORE, MCINTIRE, NELSON, PHAM H, WISE (at the re-
quest of Oregon Public Education Network) (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: Allows learning by playing to count towards time required by the State Board of Edu-
cation. (Flesch Readability Score: 61.8).
Directs the State Board of Education to allow play-based learning to be considered instructional
time for students in kindergarten through grade five.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to play-based learning.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 329.
5 SECTION 2. (1) As used in this section:
6 (a) “Free play” means activities that:
7 (A) Are performed by a child for self-amusement; and
8 (B) Are spontaneous, voluntary, unstructured and child-directed.
9 (b) “Guided play” means experiences that:
10 (A) Are under adult guidance; and
11 (B) Combine the child-directed nature of free play with a focus on learning outcomes.
12 (c) “Play-based learning” means learning that:
13 (A) Emphasizes free play or guided play; and
14 (B) Uses developmentally appropriate strategies that can be integrated into existing
15 learning standards.
16 (2) Any rules adopted by the State Board of Education that prescribe requirements for
17 instructional time shall allow play-based learning to be considered instructional time for
18 students in kindergarten through grade five.
19 (3) For the purpose of this section, play-based learning does not include participation in
20 physical education that complies with the requirements of ORS 329.496 or recess.
