SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: This Act creates a program to recover the costs of climate change. (Flesch Readability
Score: 81.8).
Establishes the Climate Superfund Cost Recovery Program as an interagency response to the
effects of climate change. Directs the Department of Land Conservation and Development to serve
as the lead agency of an interagency team. Requires the department to conduct an assessment on
the costs of greenhouse gas emissions.
Directs the Department of Environmental Quality to issue cost recovery demand notices to en-
tities determined to be responsible for the costs of climate change.
Establishes the Climate Superfund Cost Recovery Program Account.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.

21 adapt to and mitigate the effects of climate change and hold responsible parties strictly liable
22 for the costs of climate change caused by covered greenhouse gas emissions.
23 (2)(a) The Department of Land Conservation and Development shall lead an interagency

27 (c) The Department of Land Conservation and Development and other state agencies
28 participating in the interagency team may enter into intergovernmental agreements as nec-
29 essary to carry out the duties of the interagency team.
30 (3)(a) State agencies participating in the interagency team shall work collaboratively to
31 carry out the purposes of the program.
32 (b) The department shall serve as the lead agency of the interagency team and shall, in
33 coordination with the interagency team:
34 (A) Develop, adopt, implement and update a resilience implementation strategy that will
35 identify eligible climate change resilience projects; and
36 (B) Disperse funds to implement eligible climate change resilience projects.

38 team, shall develop and implement the following portions of the resilience implementation
39 strategy:
40 (A) The Oregon Department of Emergency Management shall develop and implement
41 those portions of the resilience implementation strategy related to disaster preparedness and
42 recovery.
43 (B) The Oregon Health Authority shall develop and implement those portions of the

45 change, including harms resulting from extreme heat and other extreme weather events and

16 zones, shorelands, wetlands and similar lands;
17 (b) Practices to adapt infrastructure to the impacts of climate change, including but not
18 limited to homes, buildings and public spaces;

20 response to climate-related threats;
21 (d) Practices that support economic and environmental sustainability in the face of

40 stakeholders; and

42 to the impacts of climate change, including disadvantaged, low-income and rural communi-
