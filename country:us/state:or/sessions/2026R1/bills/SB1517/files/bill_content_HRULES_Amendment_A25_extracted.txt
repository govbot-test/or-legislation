3 “SECTION 1. (1) As used in this section:
4 “(a) ‘Operator’ means a person that:
5 “(A) Offers a person the opportunity to participate in a sport, fit-
6 ness or recreational activity; or
7 “(B) Operates or provides a facility or place where a person can
8 participate in a sport, fitness or recreational activity.
9 “(b) ‘Sport, fitness or recreational activity’ means an indoor or
10 outdoor activity involving elements of inherent risk, including but not
11 limited to hunting, fishing, swimming, boating, rafting, biking, camp-
12 ing, skiing, snowboarding, winter sports, team and individual sports,
13 climbing, equestrian and rodeo activities, hiking, outfitter guiding,
14 environmental restoration and maintenance, ocean and water sports,
15 motorized recreation, athletic or fitness competitions, fitness activities
16 and training activities.
17 “(2) Except as provided in subsection (3) of this section, an operator
18 may require a participant in a sport, fitness or recreational activity
19 who is 18 years of age or older, or a parent or guardian on behalf of
20 a participant in a sport, fitness or recreational activity who is under
21 18 years of age, to release the operator from any claim for damages

1 for injuries to the participant resulting from ordinary negligence that
2 arises out of the sport, fitness or recreational activity, or out of vol-
3 unteering to maintain facilities or places used for a sport, fitness or
4 recreational activity, if the participant or parent or guardian volun-
5 tarily signs a clear and unambiguous written release disclosing risks
6 associated with the sport, fitness or recreational activity or volun-
7 teering and waiving the operator’s liability for damages for injuries
8 resulting from those risks. A release described in this subsection is
9 not per se unconscionable or void as contrary to public policy.
10 “(3) An operator may not require a person to release the operator
11 from the following types of claims:
12 “(a) Claims for greater than ordinary negligence, including gross
13 negligence, reckless conduct, willful misconduct or intentional torts.
14 “(b) Claims for negligence per se.
15 “(c) Claims relating to the maintenance or inspection of safety
16 equipment supplied by the operator and used in connection with the
17 sport, fitness or recreational activity or volunteering.
18 “(d) Claims relating to negligent safety-related training of an em-
19 ployee or agent of the operator whose conduct causes injury.
20 “(e) Claims relating to abuse, as defined in ORS 419B.005, or phys-
21 ical or sexual assault of an adult.
22 “(f) Claims relating to the negligent hiring, training, credentialing,
23 supervision or retention of an employee or agent of the operator whose
24 conduct that causes injury constitutes gross negligence, reckless con-
25 duct, willful misconduct or an intentional tort.
