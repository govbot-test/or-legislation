SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: This Act changes some laws related to the board of a community college. This Act takes
effect when the Governor signs it. (Flesch Readability Score: 72.6).
Requires the members of a board of education of a community college district to have an official
electronic mail address that is posted on a publicly accessible website. Requires the board to include
at least one nonvoting member of the board who is a student at a community college in the district.
Requires the board to report to the Legislative Assembly on the board’s compliance with certain
provisions of the Act. Modifies requirements for filling vacancies on the board.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.

4 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
5 SECTION 1. ORS 341.283 is amended to read:
6 341.283. (1) After July 1 of each year, the board of a district shall meet and organize by electing
7 a chairperson and a vice chairperson from its members.
8 (2) The board shall provide for the time and place of its regular meetings, at any of which it
9 may adjourn to the next succeeding regular meeting or to some specified time prior thereto. Special
10 meetings shall be convened by order of the chairperson of the board or upon the request of four
11 board members at least 24 hours before such meeting is to be held, or by common consent of the
12 board members. Notice of any special meeting shall be given to the members pursuant to bylaws of
13 the board.
14 (3) A majority of the board members shall constitute a quorum. The affirmative vote of the ma-
15 jority of members of the board is required to transact any business.
16 (4) The board shall adopt rules for the government of the conduct of its members and its pro-
17 ceedings. The board shall keep a journal and, on the call of any one of its members, shall cause the
18 yeas and nays to be taken and entered upon its journal upon any question before it.
19 (5) Any duty imposed upon the board as a body shall be performed at a regular or special
20 meeting and shall be made a matter of record. The consent to any particular measure obtained from
21 individual board members when the board is not in session shall not be an act of the board and shall
22 not be binding upon the district.
23 (6) Members of the board shall receive no compensation for their services, but they shall be al-
24 lowed the actual and necessary expenses incurred by them in the performance of their duties.
25 (7) Each member of the board shall have an official institutional electronic mail address
26 that is posted on a publicly available website for the board.

9 SECTION 8. ORS 341.335 is amended to read:
10 341.335. (1) The board shall declare the office of a board member vacant if it finds any of the
11 following:
12 (a) The incumbent has died or resigned.
13 (b) The incumbent has been removed or recalled from office or the election of the incumbent
14 thereto has been declared void by the judgment of a court.
15 (c) The incumbent has ceased to be a resident of the district from which the incumbent was
16 nominated or elected.
17 (d) The incumbent has ceased to discharge the duties of office for two consecutive months unless
18 prevented therefrom by sickness or other unavoidable cause or unless excused by the chairperson
19 of the board.
20 (2) A board member who is nominated or elected by zone and who changes permanent residence
21 from one zone of a district to another zone or who by a change in zone boundaries no longer resides
22 in the zone of nomination or election is entitled to continue to serve as board member until June
23 30 following the next regular district election at which a successor shall be elected by the electors
24 to serve for the remainder of the unexpired term, if any. The successor shall take office July 1 next
25 following the election.
26 (3) When a vacancy is declared under subsection (1) of this section, and the vacancy occurs
27 not less than 90 days before the regular district election for the position, the remaining board
28 members shall meet and appoint a person to fill the vacancy from any of the electors of the district
29 not more than 90 days after the vacancy occurs, if the position is one filled by both nomination
30 and election at-large, and otherwise from any of the electors of the zone from which the vacancy
31 occurs.
32 (4) [If the offices of a majority of the board members are vacant at the same time,] The governing
33 body of the principal county shall appoint persons to fill the vacancies from any of the electors of
34 the district if the positions are filled by both nomination and election at-large, and otherwise from
35 any of the electors of the zone from which the vacancy occurs if:
36 (a) The offices of a majority of the board members are vacant at the same time; or
37 (b) The remaining board members fail to appoint a person to fill a vacancy in accordance
38 with subsection (3) of this section. The governing body shall make an appointment under
39 this paragraph not more than 10 days following the failure of the board to fill a vacancy.
40 (5) The period of service of a board member appointed under subsection (3) or (4) of this section
41 commences upon appointment and expires June 30 next following the next regular district election
42 at which a successor is elected. The successor shall be elected to serve the remainder, if any, of the
43 term for which the appointment was made. If the term for which the appointment was made expires
44 June 30 after the election of the successor, the successor shall be elected to a full term. In either
45 case, the successor shall take office on July 1.
