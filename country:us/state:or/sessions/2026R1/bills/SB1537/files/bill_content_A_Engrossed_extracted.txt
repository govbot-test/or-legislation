SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: This Act changes some laws related to the board of a community college. This Act takes
effect when the Governor signs it. (Flesch Readability Score: 72.6).
Requires the members of a board of education of a community college district to have an official
electronic mail address that is posted on a publicly accessible website. Requires the board to include
at least one nonvoting member of the board who is a student at a community college in the district.
Requires the board to report to the Legislative Assembly on the board’s compliance with certain
provisions of the Act. Modifies requirements for filling vacancies on the board.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.

9 may adjourn to the next succeeding regular meeting or to some specified time prior thereto. Special

11 board members at least 24 hours before such meeting is to be held, or by common consent of the
12 board members. Notice of any special meeting shall be given to the members pursuant to bylaws of
13 the board.
14 (3) A majority of the board members shall constitute a quorum. The affirmative vote of the ma-

20 meeting and shall be made a matter of record. The consent to any particular measure obtained from
21 individual board members when the board is not in session shall not be an act of the board and shall
22 not be binding upon the district.

6 SECTION 3. ORS 341.275 is amended to read:
7 341.275. [(1) The board shall be composed of seven members.]
8 [(2) No person who is an employee of the community college district shall be eligible to serve as a
9 member of the board for the district by which the employee is employed.]
10 (1) The board shall be composed of seven voting members and at least one nonvoting
11 member who is a student enrolled in at least six in-person credits per term, other than a
12 summer term, at a community college in the district.
13 (2) A nonvoting member who is a student must be:
14 (a) Elected or selected through a process held by the student government or student
15 governance structure at a community college in the district;
16 (b) Permitted to vote ceremonially on any question subject to a vote of the board, with
17 the vote entered along with the yeas and nays in the journal of the board under ORS 341.283;
18 (c) Permitted to participate in any meeting, discussion or function of the board; and
19 (d) Provided any information distributed to voting members of the board.
20 (3) A person may not serve on the board of a specific community college district if the
21 person is an employee of the district.
22 (4)(a) Notwithstanding subsection (2)(c) of this section, a nonvoting student member of
23 the board may participate in an executive session of the board only to the extent rules
24 adopted by the board under this subsection authorize the participation of the student mem-
25 ber in the executive session.
26 (b) Each board shall adopt rules that govern a nonvoting student member’s participation
27 in executive sessions of the board.
28 SECTION 4. The amendments to ORS 341.275 by section 3 of this 2026 Act become oper-
29 ative on July 1, 2027.

15 SECTION 8. ORS 341.335 is amended to read:
16 341.335. (1) The board shall declare the office of a board member vacant if it finds any of the
17 following:
18 (a) The incumbent has died or resigned.
19 (b) The incumbent has been removed or recalled from office or the election of the incumbent
20 thereto has been declared void by the judgment of a court.
21 (c) The incumbent has ceased to be a resident of the district from which the incumbent was
22 nominated or elected.
23 (d) The incumbent has ceased to discharge the duties of office for two consecutive months unless
24 prevented therefrom by sickness or other unavoidable cause or unless excused by the chairperson
25 of the board.
26 (2) A board member who is nominated or elected by zone and who changes permanent residence
27 from one zone of a district to another zone or who by a change in zone boundaries no longer resides
28 in the zone of nomination or election is entitled to continue to serve as board member until June
29 30 following the next regular district election at which a successor shall be elected by the electors
30 to serve for the remainder of the unexpired term, if any. The successor shall take office July 1 next
31 following the election.
32 (3) When a vacancy is declared under subsection (1) of this section, and the vacancy occurs
33 not less than 90 days before the regular district election for the position, the remaining board
34 members shall meet and appoint a person to fill the vacancy from any of the electors of the district
35 not more than 90 days after the vacancy occurs, if the position is one filled by both nomination
36 and election at-large, and otherwise from any of the electors of the zone from which the vacancy
37 occurs.
38 (4)(a) If the offices of a majority of the board members are vacant at the same time, the gov-
39 erning body of the principal county shall appoint persons to fill the vacancies from any of the
40 electors of the district if the positions are filled by both nomination and election at-large, and oth-
41 erwise from any of the electors of the zone from which the vacancy occurs.
42 (b) If the remaining board members fail to appoint a person to fill a vacancy in accord-
43 ance with subsection (3) of this section:
44 (A) For at-large seats, the governing body of the county with the most electors in the
45 district shall appoint a resident of the district within the county to fill the vacancy; and

2 the most electors in the zone shall appoint a resident of the zone who is within the county.
3 (c) A governing body described in paragraph (b) of this subsection shall make an ap-
4 pointment under this subsection not more than 14 days following the failure of the board to
5 fill a vacancy.
6 (5) The period of service of a board member appointed under subsection (3) or (4) of this section
7 commences upon appointment and expires June 30 next following the next regular district election
8 at which a successor is elected. The successor shall be elected to serve the remainder, if any, of the
9 term for which the appointment was made. If the term for which the appointment was made expires
10 June 30 after the election of the successor, the successor shall be elected to a full term. In either
11 case, the successor shall take office on July 1.
12 SECTION 9. This 2026 Act being necessary for the immediate preservation of the public

14 on its passage.
