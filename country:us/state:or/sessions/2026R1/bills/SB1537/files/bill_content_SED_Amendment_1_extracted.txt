SB 1537-1
(LC 105)
2/9/26 (VSR/DJ/ps)
Requested by SENATE COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION
PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO
1 On page 2 of the printed bill, delete lines 3 through 21 and insert:
2 “SECTION 3. ORS 341.275 is amended to read:
3 “341.275. [(1) The board shall be composed of seven members.]
4 “[(2) No person who is an employee of the community college district shall
5 be eligible to serve as a member of the board for the district by which the
6 employee is employed.] (1) The board shall be composed of seven voting
7 members and at least one nonvoting member who is a student enrolled
8 in at least six in-person credits per term, other than a summer term,
9 at a community college in the district.
10 “(2) A nonvoting member who is a student must be:
11 “(a) Elected or selected through a process held by the student gov-
12 ernment or student governance structure at a community college in
13 the district;
14 “(b) Permitted to vote ceremonially on any question subject to a
15 vote of the board, with the vote entered along with the yeas and nays
16 in the journal of the board under ORS 341.283;
17 “(c) Permitted to participate in any meeting, discussion or function
18 of the board; and
19 “(d) Provided any information distributed to voting members of the
20 board.
21 “(3) A person may not serve on the board of a specific community
1 college district if the person is an employee of the district.
2 “(4)(a) Notwithstanding subsection (2)(c) of this section, a nonvot-
3 ing student member of the board may participate in an executive ses-
4 sion of the board only to the extent rules adopted by the board under
5 this subsection authorize the participation of the student member in
6 the executive session.
7 “(b) Each board shall adopt rules that govern a nonvoting student
8 member’s participation in executive sessions of the board.”.
9 On page 3, delete lines 9 through 45 and insert:
10 “SECTION 8. ORS 341.335 is amended to read:
11 “341.335. (1) The board shall declare the office of a board member vacant
12 if it finds any of the following:
13 “(a) The incumbent has died or resigned.
14 “(b) The incumbent has been removed or recalled from office or the
15 election of the incumbent thereto has been declared void by the judgment
16 of a court.
17 “(c) The incumbent has ceased to be a resident of the district from which
18 the incumbent was nominated or elected.
19 “(d) The incumbent has ceased to discharge the duties of office for two
20 consecutive months unless prevented therefrom by sickness or other una-
21 voidable cause or unless excused by the chairperson of the board.
22 “(2) A board member who is nominated or elected by zone and who
23 changes permanent residence from one zone of a district to another zone or
24 who by a change in zone boundaries no longer resides in the zone of nomi-
25 nation or election is entitled to continue to serve as board member until
26 June 30 following the next regular district election at which a successor
27 shall be elected by the electors to serve for the remainder of the unexpired
28 term, if any. The successor shall take office July 1 next following the
29 election.
30 “(3) When a vacancy is declared under subsection (1) of this section, and
SB 1537-1 2/9/26
Proposed Amendments to SB 1537 Page 2
1 the vacancy occurs not less than 90 days before the regular district
2 election for the position, the remaining board members shall meet and
3 appoint a person to fill the vacancy from any of the electors of the district
4 not more than 90 days after the vacancy occurs, if the position is one
5 filled by both nomination and election at-large, and otherwise from any of
6 the electors of the zone from which the vacancy occurs.
7 “(4)(a) If the offices of a majority of the board members are vacant at the
8 same time, the governing body of the principal county shall appoint persons
9 to fill the vacancies from any of the electors of the district if the positions
10 are filled by both nomination and election at-large, and otherwise from any
11 of the electors of the zone from which the vacancy occurs.
12 “(b) If the remaining board members fail to appoint a person to fill
13 a vacancy in accordance with subsection (3) of this section:
14 “(A) For at-large seats, the governing body of the county with the
15 most electors in the district shall appoint a resident of the district
16 within the county to fill the vacancy; and
17 “(B) For a zone seat for which there is a vacancy, the governing
18 body of the county with the most electors in the zone shall appoint a
19 resident of the zone who is within the county.
20 “(c) A governing body described in paragraph (b) of this subsection
21 shall make an appointment under this subsection not more than 14
22 days following the failure of the board to fill a vacancy.
23 “(5) The period of service of a board member appointed under subsection
24 (3) or (4) of this section commences upon appointment and expires June 30
25 next following the next regular district election at which a successor is
26 elected. The successor shall be elected to serve the remainder, if any, of the
27 term for which the appointment was made. If the term for which the ap-
28 pointment was made expires June 30 after the election of the successor, the
29 successor shall be elected to a full term. In either case, the successor shall
30 take office on July 1.”.
