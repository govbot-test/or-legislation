18 musical, comedic, theatrical, sports or other artistic, athletic or entertainment performance,

20 facility located in the Rose Quarter.

25 velopment Agreement between Rip City Management LLC and the City of Portland, dated
26 September 19, 2024.

7 “SECTION 3. (1) The Oregon Arena Fund is established in the State Treasury, separate
8 and distinct from the General Fund. Interest earned by the Oregon Arena Fund must be
9 credited to the fund. The Oregon Arena Fund consists of moneys appropriated, allocated,
10 deposited or transferred to the fund by the Legislative Assembly or from any public or pri-
11 vate source and interest earned on moneys in the fund. The moneys in the Oregon Arena
12 Fund are continuously appropriated to the Oregon Department of Administrative Services
13 for the purpose of paying expenses of the joint authority, including, without limitation, ex-
14 penses related to construction, renovation, capital improvements, repair, maintenance, de-
15 ferred maintenance, arena operations and debt service, and subject to subsection (3) of this
16 section.
17 “(2) The department and the State Treasurer are authorized to create subaccounts within
18 the fund as necessary or convenient for the administration of the fund.
19 “(3) The department and the State Treasurer shall ensure that the following moneys are
20 separately accounted for and used as follows:
21 “(a) Proceeds of debt instruments issued by the State of Oregon and deposited in the fund
22 may be used only for construction and renovation of the Moda Center and debt service; and
23 “(b) Moneys deposited in the fund by Multnomah County may be used only for con-
24 struction, renovation, maintenance and deferred maintenance of the Moda Center and debt
25 service.
26 “(4) The State of Oregon and the Legislative Assembly do not have a legal obligation to
27 deposit moneys in the fund, appropriate moneys to the fund or otherwise make moneys
28 available to the fund. The Legislative Assembly declares its current intention to issue debt
29 instruments sufficient to yield $365,000,000 in net proceeds to support construction and ren-
30 ovation of the Moda Center.
