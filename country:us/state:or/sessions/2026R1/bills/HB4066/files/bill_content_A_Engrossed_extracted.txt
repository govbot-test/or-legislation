SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: Changes laws related to the OHSU board, school board stipends, the ICEOMC,
rules of ODE, school district budgets, a study by PSU and lamps used by ESDs. (Flesch
Readability Score: 63.4).
[Digest: Changes laws related to the OHSU board, stipends for ESDs and school districts, terms
in the ICEOMC and rules of ODE. (Flesch Readability Score: 68.6).]
Prohibits the student member of the Oregon Health and Science University Board of Directors
from participating in any discussions or action by the board or attending any executive session of
the board involving collective bargaining issues that affect students. Requires the board to meet at
least four times per year.
Allows directors of education service district boards to receive stipends. Specifies when adjust-
ments for inflation can be made for stipends of school districts.
Changes the definition for the term “active duty” in the Interstate Compact on Educational
Opportunity for Military Children.
Clarifies that the rulemaking authority for the Department of Education is by the State Board
of Education.
Allows a school district to not include a member of the educational equity advisory
committee on the budget committee if no member of the educational equity advisory com-
mittee is willing or able to serve on the budget committee.
Directs Portland State University instead of the Higher Education Coordinating Com-
mission to report to the Legislative Assembly on a study examining the labor standards and
working experiences of farmworkers in this state and evaluating the adequacy and avail-
ability of existing workplace protections for farmworkers.
Allows education service districts to be temporarily exempt from certain requirements
applicable to lamps in buildings used to provide educational services to students.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.

20 ARTICLE I
21 PURPOSE
22
23
24 It is the purpose of this compact to remove barriers to educational success imposed on children of
25 military families because of frequent moves and deployment of their parents by:
26 A. Facilitating the timely enrollment of children of military families and ensuring that they are
27 not placed at a disadvantage due to difficulty in the transfer of education records from a previous
28 school district or variations in entrance or age requirements.
29 B. Facilitating the student placement process through which children of military families are
30 not disadvantaged by variations in attendance requirements, scheduling, sequencing, grading, course
31 content or assessment.
32 C. Facilitating the qualification and eligibility for enrollment, educational programs and partic-
33 ipation in extracurricular academic, athletic and social activities.
34 D. Facilitating the on-time graduation of children of military families.
35 E. Providing for the promulgation and enforcement of administrative rules implementing the
36 provisions of this compact.
37 F. Providing for the uniform collection and sharing of information between and among member
38 states, schools and military families under this compact.
39 G. Promoting coordination between this compact and other compacts affecting military children.
40 H. Promoting flexibility and cooperation between the educational system, parents and the stu-
41 dent in order to achieve educational success for the student.

22 ARTICLE V
23 PLACEMENT AND ATTENDANCE
24
25 A. Course placement. When the student transfers before or during the school year, the receiving
26 state school shall initially honor placement of the student in educational courses based on the
27 student’s enrollment in the sending state school or educational assessments conducted at the school
28 in the sending state if the courses are offered. Course placement includes but is not limited to
29 honors, International Baccalaureate, advanced placement, vocational, technical and career pathways
30 courses. Continuing the student’s academic program from the previous school and promoting place-
31 ment in academically and career challenging courses should be paramount when considering place-
32 ment. This does not preclude the school in the receiving state from performing subsequent
33 evaluations to ensure appropriate placement and continued enrollment of the student in a course.
34 B. Educational program placement. The receiving state school shall initially honor placement
35 of the student in educational programs based on current educational assessments conducted at the
36 school in the sending state or based on participation or placement in like programs in the sending
37 state. Such programs include, but are not limited to, talented and gifted programs and English as a
38 second language programs. This does not preclude the school in the receiving state from performing
39 subsequent evaluations to ensure appropriate placement of the student.
40 C. Special education services.
41 1. In compliance with the federal requirements of the Individuals with Disabilities Education
42 Act, 20 U.S.C. 1400 et seq., the receiving state shall initially provide comparable services to a stu-
43 dent with disabilities based on the student’s current individualized education program. This does not
44 preclude the school in the receiving state from performing subsequent evaluations to ensure appro-
45 priate placement of the student.

17 ARTICLE VI
18 ELIGIBILITY
19
20 A. Eligibility for enrollment.
21 1. Special power of attorney, relative to the guardianship of a child of a military family and
22 executed under applicable law, shall be sufficient for the purposes of enrollment and all other
23 actions requiring parental participation and consent.
24 2. A local education agency shall be prohibited from charging local tuition to a transitioning
25 military child placed in the care of a noncustodial parent or other person standing in loco parentis
26 who lives in a jurisdiction other than that of the custodial parent.
27 3. A transitioning military child, placed in the care of a noncustodial parent or other person
28 standing in loco parentis who lives in a jurisdiction other than that of the custodial parent, may
29 continue to attend the school in which the child was enrolled while residing with the custodial
30 parent.
31 B. Eligibility for extracurricular participation. State and local education agencies shall facilitate
32 the opportunity for transitioning military children’s inclusion in extracurricular activities, regard-
33 less of application deadlines, to the extent they are otherwise qualified.

SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: Changes laws related to the OHSU board, school board stipends, the ICEOMC,
rules of ODE, school district budgets, a study by PSU and lamps used by ESDs. (Flesch
Readability Score: 63.4).
[Digest: Changes laws related to the OHSU board, stipends for ESDs and school districts, terms
in the ICEOMC and rules of ODE. (Flesch Readability Score: 68.6).]
Prohibits the student member of the Oregon Health and Science University Board of Directors
from participating in any discussions or action by the board or attending any executive session of
the board involving collective bargaining issues that affect students. Requires the board to meet at
least four times per year.
Allows directors of education service district boards to receive stipends. Specifies when adjust-
ments for inflation can be made for stipends of school districts.
Changes the definition for the term “active duty” in the Interstate Compact on Educational
Opportunity for Military Children.
Clarifies that the rulemaking authority for the Department of Education is by the State Board
of Education.
Allows a school district to not include a member of the educational equity advisory
committee on the budget committee if no member of the educational equity advisory com-
mittee is willing or able to serve on the budget committee.
Directs Portland State University instead of the Higher Education Coordinating Com-
mission to report to the Legislative Assembly on a study examining the labor standards and
working experiences of farmworkers in this state and evaluating the adequacy and avail-
ability of existing workplace protections for farmworkers.
Allows education service districts to be temporarily exempt from certain requirements
applicable to lamps in buildings used to provide educational services to students.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.

24 It is the purpose of this compact to remove barriers to educational success imposed on children of

26 A. Facilitating the timely enrollment of children of military families and ensuring that they are

29 B. Facilitating the student placement process through which children of military families are
30 not disadvantaged by variations in attendance requirements, scheduling, sequencing, grading, course
31 content or assessment.
32 C. Facilitating the qualification and eligibility for enrollment, educational programs and partic-
33 ipation in extracurricular academic, athletic and social activities.
34 D. Facilitating the on-time graduation of children of military families.
35 E. Providing for the promulgation and enforcement of administrative rules implementing the
36 provisions of this compact.
37 F. Providing for the uniform collection and sharing of information between and among member
38 states, schools and military families under this compact.
39 G. Promoting coordination between this compact and other compacts affecting military children.
40 H. Promoting flexibility and cooperation between the educational system, parents and the stu-
41 dent in order to achieve educational success for the student.

22 ARTICLE V
23 PLACEMENT AND ATTENDANCE
24
25 A. Course placement. When the student transfers before or during the school year, the receiving
26 state school shall initially honor placement of the student in educational courses based on the
27 student’s enrollment in the sending state school or educational assessments conducted at the school
28 in the sending state if the courses are offered. Course placement includes but is not limited to
29 honors, International Baccalaureate, advanced placement, vocational, technical and career pathways
30 courses. Continuing the student’s academic program from the previous school and promoting place-

32 ment. This does not preclude the school in the receiving state from performing subsequent
33 evaluations to ensure appropriate placement and continued enrollment of the student in a course.
34 B. Educational program placement. The receiving state school shall initially honor placement
35 of the student in educational programs based on current educational assessments conducted at the
36 school in the sending state or based on participation or placement in like programs in the sending
37 state. Such programs include, but are not limited to, talented and gifted programs and English as a
38 second language programs. This does not preclude the school in the receiving state from performing
39 subsequent evaluations to ensure appropriate placement of the student.

42 Act, 20 U.S.C. 1400 et seq., the receiving state shall initially provide comparable services to a stu-

44 preclude the school in the receiving state from performing subsequent evaluations to ensure appro-

12 A. Withdrawal.
13 1. Once effective, this compact shall continue in force and remain binding upon each and every

22 Interstate Commission shall notify the other member states of the withdrawing state’s intent to

31 ber state that reduces the membership in this compact to one member state.

33 further force or effect, and the business and affairs of the Interstate Commission shall be concluded
