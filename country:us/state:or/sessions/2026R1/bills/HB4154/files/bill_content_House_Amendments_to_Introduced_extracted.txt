Section 1:
HOUSE AMENDMENTS TO
HOUSE BILL 4154
By COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION
February 16
1 On page 1 of the printed bill, line 2, after the first semicolon delete the rest of the line and line
2 3 and insert “and declaring an emergency.”.
3 Delete lines 5 through 28 and delete page 2 and insert:
4 “SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 329.
5 “SECTION 2. (1) Using student attendance data received from a school district under
6 ORS 327.133, the Department of Education shall calculate the following for students who
7 were enrolled at the school district or a public charter school for a total of at least 10 days
8 during the reporting period:
9 “(a) The total number of students in average daily membership for the school district or
10 public charter school;
11 “(b) The total number of students in average daily membership who are regular
12 attenders;
13 “(c) The percentage of students who are regular attenders;
14 “(d) The total number of students in average daily membership who are chronically ab-
15 sent; and
16 “(e) The percentage of students who are chronically absent.
17 “(2)(a) Four times per calendar year, the department shall:
18 “(A) Compile the data as provided by subsection (1) of this section;
19 “(B) Provide each school district and public charter school at least 15 business days to
20 review the compiled data and request adjustments be made to the data, in the time and
21 manner provided by rule of the State Board of Education, before the department makes the
22 data available to the public; and
23 “(C) Make available to the public the compiled data on the department’s website in an
24 easy-to-use format, including disaggregating the data by student group.
25 “(b) When making the data available to the public, the department shall clearly describe:
26 “(A) The requirements used for determining when a student is a regular attender or
27 chronically absent;
28 “(B) Whether any changes have been made in the methodology for determining when a
29 student is a regular attender or chronically absent; and
30 “(C) Guidance on how the data should be interpreted and used, including any limitations
31 of the data when making comparisons across school districts, schools and reporting periods.
32 “SECTION 3. (1) Section 2 of this 2026 Act becomes operative on July 1, 2026.
33 “(2) Notwithstanding the operative date set forth in subsection (1) of this section, the
34 State Board of Education and the Department of Education may take any action before the
35 operative date set forth in subsection (1) of this section that is necessary for the department
LC 117/HB 4154-2
1 and board to exercise, on and after the operative date set forth in subsection (1) of this
2 section, all of the duties, functions and power conferred upon the department and board by
3 section 2 of this 2026 Act.
4 “SECTION 4. (1) The reporting requirements and the data compilation requirements of
5 section 2 of this 2026 Act first apply to the 2026-2027 school year.
6 “(2) The Department of Education shall include on the department’s website a clear de-
7 scription of the changes that have been made in the methodology for determining when a
8 student is a regular attender or chronically absent for the 2026-2027 school year in compar-
9 ison to the methodology used in making that determination for the 2025-2026 school year.
10 “SECTION 5. This 2026 Act being necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
11 peace, health and safety, an emergency is declared to exist, and this 2026 Act takes effect
12 on its passage.”.
