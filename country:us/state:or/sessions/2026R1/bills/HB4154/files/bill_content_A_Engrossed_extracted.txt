SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: Makes public data that are collected about students going to school. (Flesch Readability
Score: 64.9).
Requires the compilation of data about students who are regular attenders or who are chron-
ically absent.
[Takes effect July 1, 2026.]
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to student attendance data; and declaring an emergency.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 329.
5 SECTION 2. (1) Using student attendance data received from a school district under ORS
6 327.133, the Department of Education shall calculate the following for students who were
7 enrolled at the school district or a public charter school for a total of at least 10 days during
8 the reporting period:
9 (a) The total number of students in average daily membership for the school district or
10 public charter school;
11 (b) The total number of students in average daily membership who are regular attenders;
12 (c) The percentage of students who are regular attenders;
13 (d) The total number of students in average daily membership who are chronically ab-
14 sent; and
15 (e) The percentage of students who are chronically absent.
16 (2)(a) Four times per calendar year, the department shall:
17 (A) Compile the data as provided by subsection (1) of this section;
18 (B) Provide each school district and public charter school at least 15 business days to
19 review the compiled data and request adjustments be made to the data, in the time and
20 manner provided by rule of the State Board of Education, before the department makes the
21 data available to the public; and
22 (C) Make available to the public the compiled data on the department’s website in an
23 easy-to-use format, including disaggregating the data by student group.
24 (b) When making the data available to the public, the department shall clearly describe:
