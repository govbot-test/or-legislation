Section 1:
House Bill 4120
Sponsored by Representatives EDWARDS, RUIZ; Representatives BOICE, BREESE-IVERSON, BUNCH, HELFRICH,
LEVY B, LEWIS, MCINTIRE, OSBORNE, RIEKE SMITH, SCHARF, SKARLATOS, WISE, YUNKER, Senators
SOLLMAN, WEBER (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: This Act lets landlords restrict smoking in apartments. (Flesch Readability Score: 61.2).
Allows residential landlords of multifamily housing to convert premises to nonsmoking for ex-
isting tenants upon 90 days’ written notice. Applies to new and existing rental agreements.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to smoking policies in residential tenancies; creating new provisions; and amending ORS
3 90.262 and 479.305.
4 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
5 SECTION 1. ORS 90.262 is amended to read:
6 90.262. (1) A landlord, from time to time, may adopt a rule or regulation, however described,
7 concerning the tenant’s use and occupancy of the premises. It is enforceable against the tenant only
8 if:
9 (a) Its purpose is to promote the convenience, safety or welfare of the tenants in the premises,
10 preserve the landlord’s property from abusive use, or make a fair distribution of services and facil-
11 ities held out for the tenants generally;
12 (b) It is reasonably related to the purpose for which it is adopted;
13 (c) It applies to all tenants in the premises in a fair manner;
14 (d) It is sufficiently explicit in its prohibition, direction or limitation of the tenant’s conduct to
15 fairly inform the tenant of what the tenant must or must not do to comply;
16 (e) It is not for the purpose of evading the obligations of the landlord; and
17 (f) The tenant has written notice of it at the time the tenant enters into the rental agreement,
18 or when it is adopted.
19 (2) If a rule or regulation adopted after the tenant enters into the rental agreement works a
20 substantial modification of the bargain, it is not valid unless:
21 (a) The tenant consents to it in writing[.]; or
22 (b)(A) The landlord is amending a smoking policy described in ORS 479.305 to prohibit
23 smoking within the dwelling units or within interior common areas of the premises;
24 (B) The tenancy is in a multifamily dwelling or a collection of multifamily or single-family
25 housing units planned, owned or constructed together through one or more applications or
26 development projects under ORS 227.175 or a development agreement under ORS 94.504 to
27 94.528 and developed for a project of 20 or more units;
28 (C) The change in policy does not apply to a fixed term tenancy until the end of the term;
29 (D) The landlord provides at least one area on the premises where smoking is allowed
NOTE: Matter in boldfaced type in an amended section is new; matter [italic and bracketed] is existing law to be omitted.
New sections are in boldfaced type.
LC 32
HB 4120
1 and that the landlord has posted as a designated smoking area; and
2 (E) The landlord gives written notice at least 90 days before the change in policy.
3 (3) If adopted, an occupancy guideline for a dwelling unit shall not be more restrictive than two
4 people per bedroom and shall be reasonable. Reasonableness shall be determined on a case-by-case
5 basis. Factors to be considered in determining reasonableness include, but are not limited to:
6 (a) The size of the bedrooms;
7 (b) The overall size of the dwelling unit; and
8 (c) Any discriminatory impact on those identified in ORS 659A.421.
9 (4) As used in this section:
10 (a) “Bedroom” means a habitable room that:
11 (A) Is intended to be used primarily for sleeping purposes;
12 (B) Contains at least 70 square feet; and
13 (C) Is configured so as to take the need for a fire exit into account.
14 (b) “Habitable room” means a space in a structure for living, sleeping, eating or cooking.
15 Bathrooms, toilet compartments, closets, halls, storage or utility space and similar areas are not
16 included.
17 SECTION 2. ORS 479.305 is amended to read:
18 479.305. (1) As used in this section:
19 (a) “Inhalant delivery system” and “tobacco products” have the meanings given those
20 terms in ORS 431A.175.
21 (b) “Smoking” means inhaling or possessing an activated inhalant delivery system or a
22 lit tobacco product in any manner or in any form.
23 [(1)] (2) Except as provided in subsection [(2)] (3) of this section, the rental agreement for a
24 dwelling unit regulated under ORS chapter 90 must include a disclosure of the smoking policy for
25 the premises on which the dwelling unit is located. The disclosure must state whether smoking is
26 prohibited on the premises, allowed on the entire premises or allowed in limited areas on the
27 premises. If the smoking policy allows smoking in limited areas on the premises, the disclosure must
28 identify the areas on the premises where smoking is allowed.
29 [(2)] (3) This section does not apply to a rental agreement subject to ORS 90.505 to 90.850 for
30 space in a facility as defined in ORS 90.100.
31 SECTION 3. The amendments to ORS 90.260 and 479.305 by sections 1 and 2 of this 2026
32 Act apply to rental agreements entered into before, on or after effective date of this 2026
33 Act.
