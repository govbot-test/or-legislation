Section 1:
House Bill 4033
Introduced and printed pursuant to House Rule 12.00. Presession filed (at the request of House Interim Committee
on Emergency Management and Veterans for Representative Thuy Tran)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: The Act puts ODEM in charge of the volunteer database known as ORVID. The Act tells
ODEM to adapt the database in specified ways. (Flesch Readability Score: 67.7).
Transfers responsibility for the volunteer database known as ORVID from the Higher Education
Coordinating Commission to the Oregon Department of Emergency Management. Directs the de-
partment to adapt and maintain the database.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to emergency volunteers; and declaring an emergency.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4
5 TRANSFER
6
7 SECTION 1. (1) The duties, functions and powers of the Higher Education Coordinating
8 Commission relating to the volunteer database known as ORVID are imposed upon, trans-
9 ferred to and vested in the Oregon Department of Emergency Management.
10 (2) The department shall adapt and maintain the database in a manner consistent with
11 section 9 of this 2026 Act.
12
13 RECORDS, PROPERTY, EMPLOYEES
14
15 SECTION 2. (1) The executive director of the Higher Education Coordinating Commission
16 shall:
17 (a) Deliver to the Oregon Department of Emergency Management all records and prop-
18 erty within the jurisdiction of the commission that relate to the duties, functions and powers
19 transferred by section 1 of this 2026 Act; and
20 (b) Transfer to the department those employees engaged primarily in the exercise of the
21 duties, functions and powers transferred by section 1 of this 2026 Act.
22 (2) The Director of the Oregon Department of Emergency Management shall take pos-
23 session of the records and property, and shall take charge of the employees and employ them
24 in the exercise of the duties, functions and powers transferred by section 1 of this 2026 Act,
25 without reduction of compensation but subject to change or termination of employment or
26 compensation as provided by law.
27 (3) The Governor shall resolve any dispute between the commission and the department
28 relating to transfers of records, property and employees under this section, and the

17 ACTION, PROCEEDING, PROSECUTION
18
19 SECTION 4. The transfer of duties, functions and powers to the Oregon Department of
20 Emergency Management by section 1 of this 2026 Act does not affect any action, proceeding
21 or prosecution involving or with respect to the duties, functions and powers begun before
22 and pending at the time of the transfer, except that the department is substituted for the
23 Higher Education Coordinating Commission in the action, proceeding or prosecution.
24
25 LIABILITY, DUTY, OBLIGATION
26
27 SECTION 5. (1) Nothing in sections 1 to 4 of this 2026 Act relieves a person of a liability,
28 duty or obligation accruing under or with respect to the duties, functions and powers
29 transferred by section 1 of this 2026 Act. The Oregon Department of Emergency Management
30 may undertake the collection or enforcement of any such liability, duty or obligation.
31 (2) The rights and obligations of the Higher Education Coordinating Commission legally
32 incurred under contracts, leases and business transactions executed, entered into or begun
33 before the operative date of section 1 of this 2026 Act accruing under or with respect to the
34 duties, functions and powers transferred by section 1 of this 2026 Act are transferred to the
35 department. For the purpose of succession to these rights and obligations, the department
36 is a continuation of the commission and not a new authority.
37
38 RULES
39
40 SECTION 6. Notwithstanding the transfer of duties, functions and powers by section 1
41 of this 2026 Act, the rules of the Higher Education Coordinating Commission with respect to
42 such duties, functions or powers that are in effect on the operative date of section 1 of this
43 2026 Act continue in effect until superseded or repealed by rules of the Oregon Department
44 of Emergency Management. References in the rules of the commission to the commission
45 or an officer or employee of the commission are considered to be references to the depart-

1 ment or an officer or employee of the department.
2 SECTION 7. Whenever, in any uncodified law or resolution of the Legislative Assembly
3 or in any rule, document, record or proceeding authorized by the Legislative Assembly, in
4 the context of the duties, functions and powers transferred by section 1 of this 2026 Act,
5 reference is made to the Higher Education Coordinating Commission, or an officer or em-
6 ployee of the commission whose duties, functions or powers are transferred by section 1 of
7 this 2026 Act, the reference is considered to be a reference to the Oregon Department of
8 Emergency Management, or an officer or employee of the department who by this 2026 Act
9 is charged with carrying out the duties, functions and powers.

11 OPERATIVE DATE
12
13 SECTION 8. (1) The Director of the Oregon Department of Emergency Management shall
14 notify the standing or interim committees of the Legislative Assembly and the Legislative
15 Counsel when the transfer described in section 1 of this 2026 Act is complete.
16 (2) Sections 1 to 7 of this 2026 Act become operative on the day after the date the no-
17 tifications described in subsection (1) of this section are complete.
18
19 DUTIES OF OREGON DEPARTMENT OF EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT
20
21 SECTION 9. (1) The Oregon Department of Emergency Management shall maintain a
22 statewide registry of persons who are willing and able to volunteer to perform emergency
23 service activities, as defined in ORS 401.358. The registry may include:
24 (a) Qualified emergency service volunteers, as defined in ORS 401.358;
25 (b) Persons registered as volunteers with a local government, as defined in ORS 174.116,
26 or a tribal entity, including members of community emergency response teams and neigh-
27 borhood emergency teams;
28 (c) Persons experienced in construction or other skilled trades;
29 (d) Persons certified by the State Fire Marshal as building evaluators under the Oregon
30 Safety Assessment Program established under ORS 401.256; and
31 (e) Other persons that the department determines are able to assist in emergency re-
32 sponse and recovery.
33 (2) As a condition of inclusion on the registry, the department may require:
34 (a) Evidence of relevant qualifications; and
35 (b) Training related to emergency response and recovery, including incident management
36 or command.
37 (3) The registry must include, for each person:
38 (a) The person’s name and contact information;
39 (b) The physical location where the person is based; and
40 (c) Specialized training or skills related to emergency response and recovery that the
41 person has.
42 (4) The registry must be accessible to emergency management agencies and emergency
43 program managers described in ORS 401.305. The department may make the registry ac-
44 cessible to any other persons or entities if the department determines that providing such
45 access will enhance public safety.
[3]
HB 4033
1 (5) The department shall establish procedures for collaborating with appropriate entities,
2 including the Higher Education Coordinating Commission, the Department of Human Ser-
3 vices, the Oregon Health Authority and local governments, for the purposes of:
4 (a) Gathering information for the registry, integrating data from existing databases into
5 the registry and regularly updating the information; and
6 (b) Recruiting, training, mobilizing and deploying volunteers for emergency management
7 purposes.
8 (6) The Oregon Department of Emergency Management is authorized to issue badges to
9 volunteers listed in the registry that describe a volunteer’s skills, training, licenses, certif-
10 ications or other information relevant to the performance of emergency service activities.

12 CAPTIONS
13
14 SECTION 10. The unit captions used in this 2026 Act are provided only for the conven-
15 ience of the reader and do not become part of the statutory law of this state or express any
16 legislative intent in the enactment of this 2026 Act.
17
18 EFFECTIVE DATE
19
20 SECTION 11. This 2026 Act being necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
21 peace, health and safety, an emergency is declared to exist, and this 2026 Act takes effect
22 on its passage.
