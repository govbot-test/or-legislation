House Bill 4103
Ordered by the House February 20
Including House Amendments dated February 20
Sponsored by Representative RIEKE SMITH, Senator REYNOLDS, Representative NGUYEN D; Representatives
ANDERSEN, BOWMAN, LEVY B, Senator SOLLMAN (Presession filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: The Act makes a commission on AI to look at the state’s use of AI. The Act also
tells DAS to hire a person to make a privacy, data protection and AI plan for the state.
(Flesch Readability Score: 80.4).
[Digest: The Act makes a commission on AI to be a central resource on using AI in this state.
The Act also tells DAS to hire a Chief Artificial Intelligence Officer. (Flesch Readability Score:

67.5).]
Establishes the Senator Aaron Woods Commission on Artificial Intelligence within the office of
Enterprise Information Services. [Establishes the commission’s purpose to serve as a central resource
to monitor the use of artificial intelligence technologies and systems in this state and report on long-
term policy implications.] Directs the commission to identify and report on long-term policy
implications in the use of artificial intelligence technologies by the state. Directs the commis-
sion to [provide] submit an annual report to the Legislative Assembly. Allows the commission to
make recommendations for legislation, regulations or policy to the Legislative Assembly.
Directs the Oregon Department of Administrative Services to hire [a Chief Artificial Intelligence
Officer to assist the commission in carrying out the commission’s duties.] an individual responsible
for developing the state’s strategic vision related to privacy, data protection and artificial
intelligence. Allows the department to charge a state [or local agency or public body] agency, of-
ficer, board or commission for [the commission’s] services provided by the department or the
Senator Aaron Woods Commission on Artificial Intelligence.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to artificial intelligence.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. (1) The Senator Aaron Woods Commission on Artificial Intelligence is es-
5 tablished within the office of Enterprise Information Services. The purpose of the commis-
6 sion is to identify and report on long-term policy implications in the use of artificial
7 intelligence technologies by the state government of Oregon to assist policymakers in mak-
8 ing strategic, well-informed decisions that foster innovation while safeguarding the well-
9 being of Oregonians.
10 (2) The commission shall:
11 (a) Conduct literature reviews of public policy issues around the use by state government
12 of artificial intelligence technologies, including the benefits and risks to the broader public
13 and historically disadvantaged communities, taking into consideration equity, workforce im-
14 pacts and ethical concerns;
15 (b) Conduct reviews of existing protections under federal and state laws of individuals’
16 data and privacy rights, civil rights, intellectual property rights and safety, and how federal
17 and state laws that apply to artificial intelligence technologies align, differ, conflict or

New sections are in boldfaced type.

1 interact;
2 (c) Identify uses of artificial intelligence systems by the state that present or create a
3 high risk to individuals, such as risks to individuals’ safety, employment rights, civil rights
4 or constitutional rights;
5 (d) Provide recommendations on the use of artificial intelligence by the state;
6 (e) Identify impacts from the use of artificial intelligence systems by the state on privacy
7 interests of individuals in this state and what policies are needed to protect individuals’ pri-
8 vacy interests from any diminution caused by the use of artificial intelligence systems in the
9 public sector;
10 (f) Identify impacts and opportunities related to the use of artificial intelligence systems
11 on public sector jobs and incomes in this state and recommend policies to protect public
12 sector jobs and incomes from adverse effects from the use of artificial intelligence systems
13 by the state;
14 (g) Conduct a review of issues around unlawful discrimination, on the basis of race, color,
15 national origin, creed, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, age, vet-
16 eran or military status, marital status, family status or any other protected class, from the
17 use of artificial intelligence systems by the state and provide recommendations for mitigat-
18 ing or protecting against such discrimination;
19 (h) Provide recommendations on prioritizing transparency to improve the understanding
20 of the behavior and functional components of artificial intelligence systems used by the state
21 and enable the identification of performance issues, safety and privacy concerns, biases,
22 exclusionary practices and unintended outcomes;
23 (i) Conduct a review of legal and contractual protections for individual personhood, in-
24 cluding for individuals employed by the state, against the use of artificial intelligence sys-
25 tems to create an artificial replica, including an artificial replica of a person’s voice, image
26 or likeness; and
27 (j) Conduct a review on the use of artificial intelligence systems by the state to support
28 or make decisions around employment.
29 (3) The commission shall consist of 10 voting members and six nonvoting members as
30 follows:
31 (a) The Governor shall appoint, from among persons jointly recommended by the Presi-
32 dent of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives, the following voting
33 members:
34 (A) One member who represents public sector employees, including public sector em-
35 ployees who are members of organized labor unions.
36 (B) One member who represents labor, trade or professional organizations.
37 (C) One member who represents the public universities listed in ORS 352.002 and com-
38 munity colleges, as defined in ORS 341.005, and who has expertise in the design, effects and
39 ethical use of artificial intelligence systems.
40 (D) One member who has expertise or a professional background in artificial intelligence
41 technology.
42 (E) One member who has expertise in data security.
43 (F) One member who can reflect the interests and needs of the nine federally recognized
44 Indian tribes in Oregon, appointed in consultation with the Commission on Indian Services.
45 (G) One member who has expertise in energy systems and natural resource management.

1 (H) One member who has lived experience with a multimodal communication disorder and
2 with the use of artificial intelligence.
3 (b) The Governor shall appoint, subject to confirmation by the Senate in the manner
4 prescribed in ORS 171.562 and 171.565, the following voting members:
5 (A) One member who represents communities that are disproportionately vulnerable to
6 being harmed by algorithmic bias.
7 (B) One member who represents public safety.
8 (c) The nonvoting members shall be:
9 (A) The designee of the State Chief Information Officer, who specializes in artificial in-
10 telligence strategy and privacy concerns.
11 (B) The Commissioner of the Bureau of Labor and Industries or the commissioner’s
12 designee.
13 (C) The Attorney General or the Attorney General’s designee.
14 (D) The Secretary of State or the secretary’s designee.
15 (E) The State Treasurer or the State Treasurer’s designee.
16 (F) A representative from the Judicial Department, appointed by the Chief Justice of the
17 Supreme Court.
18 (4) The term of office of each voting member is three years. A voting member serves
19 at the pleasure of the appointing authority. Before the expiration of the term of a voting
20 member, the appointing authority shall appoint a successor whose term begins on January
21 1 of the following year. A voting member may be reappointed. If there is a vacancy for any
22 cause, the appointing authority shall make an appointment to become immediately effective
23 for the unexpired term.
24 (5)(a) The commission may establish one or more advisory committees to advise in an
25 area impacted by artificial intelligence used by the state. An advisory committee may be
26 established for an ongoing or limited duration. In establishing an advisory committee, the
27 commission shall define the advisory committee’s charge, subject area of focus and scope of
28 activity.
29 (b) Members of an advisory committee are appointed from among the voting members
30 of the commission by the chairperson of the commission, subject to approval by a majority
31 of the voting members of the commission.
32 (c) An advisory committee shall consist of no more than seven members.
33 (d) Members of an advisory committee must represent a broad and diverse cross section
34 of stakeholders in the area advised on by the advisory committee.
35 (e) One voting member of the commission may serve on an advisory committee estab-
36 lished under this subsection, as the chair of the advisory committee.
37 (6) All agencies of state government, as defined in ORS 174.111, are directed to assist the
38 commission in the performance of the duties of the commission and, to the extent permitted
39 by laws relating to confidentiality, to provide information and advice that the members of
40 the commission consider necessary to perform their duties.
41 (7) On or before September 1 of each year, the commission shall submit a report in the
42 manner provided by ORS 192.245, and may include recommendations for legislation, regu-
43 lations or policies, to the Legislative Assembly related to information management and
44 technology.
45 (8) A majority of voting members of the commission constitutes a quorum for the

1 transaction of business.
2 (9) The commission shall elect one of its voting members to serve as chairperson of the
3 commission.
4 (10) The commission shall meet at least once every three months at times and places
5 specified by the call of the chairperson or of a majority of the voting members of the com-
6 mission.
7 (11) Members of the commission shall be paid compensation and expenses as provided in
8 ORS 292.495 from such funds as may be available to the commission.
9 (12) The designee of the State Chief Information Officer shall provide resources and
10 staffing required by the commission to carry out the commission’s duties.
11 SECTION 2. Notwithstanding the term of office specified in section 1 (4) of this 2026 Act,
12 of the voting members first appointed to the Senator Aaron Woods Commission on Artificial
13 Intelligence:
14 (1) Four shall serve for a term ending December 31, 2027.
15 (2) Three shall serve for a term ending December 31, 2028.
16 (3) Three shall serve for a term ending December 31, 2029.
17 SECTION 3. (1) The Oregon Department of Administrative Services shall employ, at the
18 appointment of the State Chief Information Officer, an individual responsible for developing
19 the strategic vision for the state relating to privacy, data protection and artificial intelli-
20 gence.
21 (2) The individual employed under subsection (1) of this section shall:
22 (a) Assist and advise the Senator Aaron Woods Commission on Artificial Intelligence
23 established under section 1 of this 2026 Act; and
24 (b) Provide policy and support staff to the commission to assist the commission in car-
25 rying out the commission’s duties.
26 SECTION 4. The Oregon Department of Administrative Services may charge a fee to a
27 state agency, officer, board or commission for the cost incurred by the department or the
28 Senator Aaron Woods Commission on Artificial Intelligence in providing services related to
29 artificial intelligence systems requested by a state agency, officer, board or commission.
30 SECTION 5. In addition to and not in lieu of any other appropriation, there is appropri-
31 ated to the Oregon Department of Administrative Services, for the biennium beginning July
32 1, 2025, out of the General Fund, the amount of $_______, which may be expended by the de-
33 partment to hire additional full-time employees to enable the individual employed under
34 section 3 of this 2026 Act to carry out the individual’s duties under sections 1 and 3 of this
35 2026 Act.
