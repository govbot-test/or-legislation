PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO
HOUSE BILL 4177
1 On page 1 of the printed bill, line 2, after “192.630,” insert “192.640,
2 192.650,”.
3 On page 2, line 22, delete “governing body may not” and insert “public
4 body may not permit a governing body to”.
5 On page 3, after line 13, insert:
6 “SECTION 3. ORS 192.640 is amended to read:
7 “192.640. (1) [The governing body of] A public body shall provide for and
8 give public notice, reasonably calculated to give actual notice to interested
9 persons including news media which have requested notice, of the time and
10 place for holding regular meetings of each of its governing bodies. The
11 notice shall also include a list of the principal subjects anticipated to be
12 considered at the meeting, but this requirement shall not limit the ability
13 of a governing body to consider additional subjects.
14 “(2) If an executive session only will be held, the notice shall be given
15 to the members of the governing body, to the general public and to news
16 media which have requested notice, stating the specific provision of law au-
17 thorizing the executive session.
18 “(3) No special meeting shall be held without at least 24 hours’ notice to
19 the members of the governing body, the news media which have requested
20 notice and the general public. In case of an actual emergency, a meeting may
21 be held upon such notice as is appropriate to the circumstances, but the
1 minutes for such a meeting shall describe the emergency justifying less than
2 24 hours’ notice.
3 “SECTION 4. ORS 192.650 is amended to read:
4 “192.650. (1) [The governing body of] A public body shall provide for the
5 sound, video or digital recording or the taking of written minutes of all
6 [its] meetings for each of its governing bodies. Neither a full transcript
7 nor a full recording of the meeting is required, except as otherwise provided
8 by law, but the written minutes or recording must give a true reflection of
9 the matters discussed at the meeting and the views of the participants. All
10 minutes or recordings shall be available to the public within a reasonable
11 time after the meeting, and shall include at least the following information:
12 “(a) All members of the governing body present;
13 “(b) All motions, proposals, resolutions, orders, ordinances and measures
14 proposed and their disposition;
15 “(c) The results of all votes and, except for public bodies consisting of
16 more than 25 members unless requested by a member of that body, the vote
17 of each member by name;
18 “(d) The substance of any discussion on any matter; and
19 “(e) Subject to ORS 192.311 to 192.478 relating to public records, a refer-
20 ence to any document discussed at the meeting.
21 “(2) Minutes of executive sessions shall be kept in accordance with sub-
22 section (1) of this section. However, the minutes of a hearing held under ORS
23 332.061 shall contain only the material not excluded under ORS 332.061 (2).
24 Instead of written minutes, a record of any executive session may be kept in
25 the form of a sound or video tape or digital recording, which need not be
26 transcribed unless otherwise provided by law. If the disclosure of certain
27 material is inconsistent with the purpose for which a meeting under ORS
28 192.660 is authorized to be held, that material may be excluded from disclo-
29 sure. However, excluded materials are authorized to be examined privately
30 by a court in any legal action and the court shall determine their
