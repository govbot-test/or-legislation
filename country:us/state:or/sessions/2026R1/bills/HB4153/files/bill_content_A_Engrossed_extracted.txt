SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability standards.
Digest: Allows farm stores on farmlands. (Flesch Readability Score: 90.9).
Allows counties to approve farm stores[, instead of farm stands,] as nonfarm use on lands zoned
for farm use.

6 SECTION 2. (1) As used in this section:
7 (a)(A) “Agri-tourism activity” means an activity that is conducted by a farm operation
8 to promote the farm operation, visitors to a farm store and the sale of farm store products
9 to the public and that may also generate commercial, entertainment or educational value.
10 Such activities may include:
11 (i) Farm tours;
12 (ii) Educational exhibits or classes;
13 (iii) Crop mazes;
14 (iv) Play structures;
15 (v) Farm-to-table meals;
16 (vi) Animal petting and feeding exhibits;
17 (vii) Hay or tractor rides; or
18 (viii) Other seasonal or holiday events.
19 (B) “Agri-tourism activity” does not include using a farm store for lodging or as a
20 dwelling.
21 (b) “Farm-to-table meal” means a meal offered as part of a fee-based dining experience,
22 where food grown by a farm operation or farmers in a local agricultural area is prepared and
23 served in a manner that educates the public about agricultural production or on-site sourc-
24 ing.
25 (c) “Farm unit” means a farm operation comprising all parcels being farmed by a single
26 operation, whether the operation owns or leases the parcels.
