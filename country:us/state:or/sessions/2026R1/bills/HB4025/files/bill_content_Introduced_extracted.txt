Section 1:
House Bill 4025
Introduced and printed pursuant to House Rule 12.00. Presession filed (at the request of House Interim Committee
on Commerce and Consumer Protection for Representative Nathan Sosa)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: Allows a public utility other than a power or gas company to raise rates year-round.
Takes effect 91 days after sine die. (Flesch Readability Score: 61.0).
Allows an increase in the residential rates of a public utility to take effect from November 1 to
March 31 for public utilities other than public utilities that provide electricity or natural gas ser-
vices.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to the residential rates of public utilities; amending ORS 757.210; and prescribing an ef-
3 fective date.
4 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
5 SECTION 1. ORS 757.210 is amended to read:
6 757.210. (1)(a) Whenever any public utility files with the Public Utility Commission any rate or
7 schedule of rates stating or establishing a new rate or schedule of rates or increasing an existing
8 rate or schedule of rates, the commission may, either upon written complaint or upon the
9 commission’s own initiative, after reasonable notice, conduct a hearing to determine whether the
10 rate or schedule is fair, just and reasonable. The commission shall conduct the hearing upon written
11 complaint filed by the utility, its customer or customers, or any other proper party within 60 days
12 of the utility’s filing; provided that no hearing need be held if the particular rate change is the re-
13 sult of an automatic adjustment clause. At the hearing the utility shall bear the burden of showing
14 that the rate or schedule of rates proposed to be established or increased or changed is fair, just
15 and reasonable. The commission may not authorize a rate or schedule of rates that is not fair, just
16 and reasonable.
17 (b) As used in this subsection, “automatic adjustment clause” means a provision of a rate
18 schedule that provides for rate increases or decreases or both, without prior hearing, reflecting in-
19 creases or decreases or both in costs incurred, taxes paid to units of government or revenues earned
20 by a utility and that is subject to review by the commission at least once every two years.
21 (2)(a) Subsection (1) of this section does not apply to rate changes under an approved alternative
22 form of regulation plan, including a resource rate plan under ORS 757.212.
23 (b) Any alternative form of regulation plan shall include provisions to ensure that the plan op-
24 erates in the interests of utility customers and the public generally and results in rates that are just
25 and reasonable and may include provisions establishing a reasonable range for rate of return on
26 investment. In approving a plan, the commission shall, at a minimum, consider whether the plan:
27 (A) Promotes increased efficiencies and cost control;
28 (B) Is consistent with least-cost resources acquisition policies;
NOTE: Matter in boldfaced type in an amended section is new; matter [italic and bracketed] is existing law to be omitted.
New sections are in boldfaced type.

HB 4025
1 (C) Yields rates that are consistent with those that would be obtained following application of
2 ORS 757.269;
3 (D) Is consistent with maintenance of safe, adequate and reliable service; and
4 (E) Is beneficial to utility customers generally, for example, by minimizing utility rates.
5 (c) As used in this subsection, “alternative form of regulation plan” means a plan adopted by the
6 commission upon petition by a public utility, after notice and an opportunity for a hearing, that sets
7 rates and revenues and a method for changes in rates and revenues using alternatives to cost-of-
8 service rate regulation.
9 (d) Prior to implementing a rate change under an alternative form of regulation plan, the utility
10 shall present a report that demonstrates the calculation of any proposed rate change at a public
11 meeting of the commission.
12 (3) Except as provided in ORS 757.212, the commission, at any time, may order a utility to ap-
13 pear and establish that any, or all, of its rates in a plan authorized under subsection (2) of this
14 section are in conformity with the plan and are just and reasonable. Except as provided in ORS
15 757.212, such rates, and the alternative form of regulation plan under which the rates are set, also
16 shall be subject to complaint under ORS 756.500.
17 (4) Periodically, but not less often than every two years after the implementation of a plan re-
18 ferred to in subsection (2) of this section, the commission shall submit a report to the Legislative
19 Assembly that shows the impact of the plan on rates paid by utility customers.
20 (5) The commission and staff may consult at any time with, and provide technical assistance to,
21 utilities, their customers, and other interested parties on matters relevant to utility rates and
22 charges. If a hearing is held with respect to a rate change, the commission’s decisions shall be based
23 on the record made at the hearing.
24 (6) The commission may adjust rates to mitigate an increase in residential customer rates if the
25 increase is of such magnitude that, if applied at the higher rate or all at one time, the increase
26 would affect the ability of residential customers to maintain adequate utility service. In considering
27 whether to adjust rates under this subsection, the commission:
28 (a) Must determine whether the proposed adjusted rates are fair, just and reasonable;
29 (b) May not adjust rates in a manner that results in rates that are not fair, just and reasonable;
30 and
31 (c) Shall consider and may approve deferred accounting for future cost recovery from customers
32 for those amounts that are subject to rate mitigation.
33 (7) Any increase in the residential rates of a public utility that provides electricity or na-
34 tural gas services may not take effect from November 1 to March 31.
35 SECTION 2. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
36 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.
