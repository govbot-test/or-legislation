Section 1:
Senate Bill 1556
Printed pursuant to Senate Interim Rule 213.28 by order of the President of the Senate in conformance with pre-
session filing rules, indicating neither advocacy nor opposition on the part of the President (at the request
of Chief Justice Meagan A. Flynn for Judicial Department)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: The Act changes the laws for the magistrate division of the Oregon Tax Court. (Flesch
Readability Score: 65.7).
Consolidates statutes governing the representation of parties before the magistrate division of
the Oregon Tax Court.
Applies to proceedings commenced on or after January 1, 2027.
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to representation of persons in the magistrate division of the Oregon Tax Court; creating
3 new provisions; amending ORS 305.239, 305.242 and 305.875; repealing ORS 305.245 and 305.494;
4 and prescribing an effective date.
5 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
6 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS 305.404 to
7 305.560.
8 SECTION 2. (1) Notwithstanding ORS 8.690, 9.160, 9.320 or 203.145 or ORS chapter 180,
9 and notwithstanding ORS 9.241 or any rule adopted thereunder unless the rule particularly
10 refers to the magistrate division of the Oregon Tax Court, in lieu of appearing by an attorney
11 who is an active licensee of the Oregon State Bar:
12 (a) A party to a proceeding in the magistrate division of the Oregon Tax Court may be
13 represented by a natural person designated by the party and recognized by the court as an
14 authorized representative under this section.
15 (b) A party that is represented under this subsection is bound by all things done by the
16 authorized representative and may not thereafter claim that any proceeding was legally de-
17 fective because the party was not represented by an attorney licensed to practice law in
18 Oregon.
19 (c)(A) A taxpayer that is a natural person may designate as the taxpayer’s representative
20 any other natural person, including a certified public accountant, an individual who is li-
21 censed as a real estate broker or principal real estate broker under ORS 696.022, an individ-
22 ual who is a state certified appraiser or state licensed appraiser under ORS 674.310 or is a
23 registered appraiser under ORS 308.010, a tax preparer or tax consultant licensed under ORS
24 673.605 to 673.740 or a family member of the taxpayer.
25 (B) A taxpayer that is a legal entity must designate as the taxpayer’s representative in
26 the magistrate division of the Oregon Tax Court a natural person, which may include a cer-
27 tified public accountant, an individual who is licensed as a real estate broker or principal real

26 305.242. (1)(a) When the treatment of partnership items on a partner’s return is consistent with
27 the treatment of that item on the partnership return and results in a notice of deficiency, the part-

29 venue in any conference or [before a tax court magistrate] in any proceeding with respect to the
30 administration of any tax on or measured by net income.
31 (b) Notwithstanding paragraph (a) of this subsection, a tax matters partner shall be designated
32 as provided in ORS 314.733 (2), in the case of a partnership subject to ORS 314.731 to 314.737.
33 (2) The designation of a tax matters partner shall be made in writing and filed with the de-
34 partment [or magistrate] within 30 days after the date of the notice of deficiency. The tax matters
35 partner must be:
36 (a) A general partner in the partnership at some time during the taxable year; or
37 (b) A general partner in the partnership at the time the designation is made.
38 (3) If a notice explaining the partnership adjustments is mailed by the department to the tax
39 matters partner with respect to any partnership taxable year, the tax matters partner shall supply
40 the department [or, if applicable, the magistrate] with the name, address, profits interest and taxpayer
41 identification number of each person who was a partner in the partnership at any time during the
42 taxable year, unless that information was provided in the partnership return for that year.
43 (4) A timely request for a conference filed with the department [or appeal filed with the tax
44 court] by the tax matters partner shall be considered as a request [or an appeal] by all of the part-
45 ners represented by the tax matters partner, and all issues regarding treatment of partnership items

4 partner and may not thereafter claim that any act or proceeding was legally defective because the
5 partner was not represented by an attorney.
6 SECTION 5. ORS 305.875 is amended to read:
7 305.875. In any meeting or communication with the Department of Revenue, including but not
8 limited to audits, conferences, interviews and any other meeting or communication between the
9 taxpayer and the department, the taxpayer shall have the following rights, unless waived by the
10 taxpayer:
11 (1) The right to an explanation, by an officer or employee of the department before or during
12 the meeting of:
13 (a) The audit, conference or meeting process and the taxpayer’s rights under such process; and
14 (b) The collection process and the taxpayer’s rights under such process.
15 (2) The right to make an audio recording of any meeting relating to the determination or col-
16 lection of any tax with the department representative, using the taxpayer’s own equipment, and at
17 the taxpayer’s own expense.
18 (3) If the department makes an audio recording of the meeting, the taxpayer has the right to
19 advance notice of the recording and a copy of the recording upon request. The taxpayer shall re-
20 imburse the department the reasonable cost of the copy.
21 (4) The right to consult with an attorney, certified public accountant, enrolled agent, or an other
22 person permitted to represent a taxpayer at any meeting before the department, if the taxpayer
23 clearly states to the department representative at any time during any meeting, that the taxpayer
24 wishes to consult with the person. This subsection does not apply to a meeting initiated by an ad-
25 ministrative subpoena.
26 (5) The right to be represented by anyone who is permitted to represent the taxpayer before the
27 department, as provided under ORS 305.239 and [305.245] section 2 of this 2026 Act.
28 (6) The right not to be present, if represented, at the meeting unless subpoenaed by the depart-
29 ment pursuant to ORS 305.190, or other laws of this state.
30 SECTION 6. ORS 305.245 and 305.494 are repealed.
31 SECTION 7. Section 2 of this 2026 Act, the amendments to ORS 305.239, 305.242 and
32 305.875 by sections 3 to 5 of this 2026 Act and the repeal of ORS 305.245 and 305.494 by section
33 6 of this 2026 Act apply to proceedings commenced in the magistrate division of the Oregon
34 Tax Court on or after January 1, 2027.
35 SECTION 8. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the date on which the 2026
36 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly adjourns sine die.
