Enrolled
Senate Concurrent Resolution 204
Sponsored by Senator WAGNER, Representative FAHEY, Senators SOLLMAN, GOLDEN, NASH,
SMITH DB, Representative BOICE; Senators GELSER BLOUIN, GORSEK, JAMA, LIEBER,
MANNING JR, MCLANE, PATTERSON, PROZANSKI, TAYLOR, Representatives ANDERSEN,
BUNCH, GAMBA, GOMBERG, HELM, LEVY B, LIVELY, MARSH, NELSON, OWENS, RIEKE

filed.)
Whereas Oregon’s counties serve as the backbone of this state’s democratic and governmental
systems, administering all local, state and federal elections, maintaining the integrity of Oregon’s
property tax system, collecting and distributing property taxes on behalf of all local taxing districts,
fostering access to justice by operating law libraries and partnering with the Oregon Judicial De-
partment to house the Oregon circuit court system in county courthouses; and
Whereas Oregon’s counties provide social safety net services for the most vulnerable
Oregonians, safeguarding the health and wellbeing of Oregon communities through local public
health programs, community mental health programs, Community Developmental Disabilities Pro-
grams, substance use and housing programs, workforce services and county Veteran Services Of-
fices; and
Whereas Oregon communities are kept safe through a wide assortment of county public safety
functions, including community corrections, emergency management services, sheriff’s offices, jails,
search and rescue services, marine patrols, juvenile services, criminal prosecution, emergency med-
ical services, justice courts, 911 services, medical examiners and animal control services; and
Whereas Oregon’s counties support critical public infrastructure and facilitate development to
ensure the orderly and efficient growth of Oregon communities and the preservation of Oregon’s
deeply treasured natural resources through careful maintenance of the largest share of Oregon’s
transportation system, through permitting, enforcement and inspections for Oregon’s county land use
and building code programs and through effective management of solid waste programs and the
federal Public Land Survey System in Oregon; and
Whereas in 1906, Oregon county governments formed the State Association of County Judges
and Commissioners to support and represent Oregon’s counties in legislative matters, which in 1936
evolved into the Association of Oregon Counties (AOC); and
Whereas the AOC is composed of the 36 counties in Oregon and their elected commissioners,
judges, and chairs, and these elected officials from diverse geographies and varied political and id-
eological values work together to foster nonpartisan, consensus-based policies and build solutions-
oriented partnerships with the state to guarantee effective service delivery to every Oregonian; and
Whereas for 120 years, the AOC has played a pivotal role in protecting, enhancing and em-
powering the ability of Oregon’s counties to provide essential public services to every Oregonian
and effectively address the ever-evolving needs and priorities of Oregon communities through legis-
lative advocacy, professional development, promotion of best practices and fostering collaboration
among elected officials; and
Whereas in the first century of its steadfast advocacy, the AOC played a crucial role in the
development of countless initiatives that improved the resiliency and vitality of communities
throughout Oregon; and
Whereas in the 1930s, the AOC supported the passage of early federal “Payment in Lieu of
Taxes” legislation; and

Whereas in 1947, the AOC championed the first county planning law, and much later, the 1973
Land Conservation and Development Act, which made planning and development regulation manda-
tory; and
Whereas in the 1950s, the AOC advocated for a property appraisal program that reduced ineq-
uities in Oregon’s property tax system; and
Whereas in 1961, the AOC sponsored legislation delegating to counties the power to convert to
the Board of County Commissioner system if the county judge no longer had any judicial functions;
and
Whereas also in 1961, the AOC worked to expand state shared revenues of net liquor receipts
with counties, and that initiative expanded to cigarette tax revenues in 1965 and lottery revenues
in 1991; and
Whereas from 1963 to 1966, the AOC secured double federal appropriations for National Forest
road construction; and
Whereas in 1964, the AOC generated Oregon’s first county roads inventory and needs study; and
Whereas in 1976, the AOC’s support was crucial to the passage of the National Forest Man-
agement Act; and
Whereas in 1987, the AOC created the Forest Trust Land Advisory Committee, composed of
forest trust land county commissioners who provided input on the management of Oregon forests to
the State Board of Forestry and the State Forester; and
Whereas in 1989, the AOC assisted with the creation of the County Assessment Function
Funding Assistance Account, which was a crucial service benefiting all local taxing districts and the
public; and
Whereas in 1990, the AOC created and maintained the Integrated Road Information System,
which played a critical role in county public asset management; and
Whereas in 2000, the AOC advocated in Congress for the passage of the Secure Rural Schools
and Community Self-Determination Act to stabilize revenue to counties that previously relied on
fluctuating timber revenues; and
Whereas in 2014, the AOC helped create the Regional Solutions Program, a resource for com-
munity and economic development that provides collaborative governance, regional coordination and
creative problem-solving to communities throughout Oregon; and
Whereas in 2024, the AOC partnered with state and local public safety agencies to form a new
deflection model for people suffering from substance use disorder; and
Whereas for the last two decades, the AOC has played a key role in passing state and federal
legislation to bolster intergovernmental partnership and deliver efficient and effective public ser-
vices to Oregonians, including major shared revenue-based state transportation packages that have
kept the traveling public moving and safe; now, therefore,
Be It Resolved by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Oregon:
That we, the members of the Eighty-third Legislative Assembly, recognize, honor and celebrate
the contributions and value of the Association of Oregon Counties on the 120th anniversary of its
founding, and we encourage continued collaboration between the Legislative Assembly and our
county partners on behalf of our shared constituents, the residents of Oregon.
