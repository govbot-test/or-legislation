Section 1:
House Bill 4127
Sponsored by Representatives BOWMAN, ANDERSEN, Senator PATTERSON, Representatives CHAICHI,
CHOTZEN, DOBSON, NOSSE, RIEKE SMITH, Senators CAMPOS, FREDERICK, PROZANSKI; Representatives
FRAGALA, GAMBA, GOMBERG, GRAYBER, HUDSON, JAVADI, MARSH, MUNOZ, NATHANSON, NELSON,
PHAM H, WISE, Senators GELSER BLOUIN, GOLDEN, JAMA, NERON MISSLIN, PHAM K (Presession
filed.)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: The Act allows OHA to pay health care providers that may not receive federal Medicaid
funds. The Act requires OHA to pay providers using only state funds. (Flesch Readability Score:

Section 2:
62.7).
Requires the Oregon Health Authority to adopt a payment mechanism to pay certain nonprofit
reproductive health care providers that are not eligible to receive federal Medicaid funds for ser-
vices provided to medical assistance recipients. Applies to claims for payment for services provided
on or after July 4, 2025, if the claim is not eligible for federal financial participation and has not
yet been paid.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to Medicaid payments to reproductive health care providers; and declaring an emergency.
3 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
4 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter 414.
5 SECTION 2. (1) As used in this section, “prohibited entity” means a nonprofit reproduc-
6 tive health care provider that is:
7 (a) Enrolled in the state medical assistance program; and
8 (b) Not eligible to receive federal Medicaid funds.
9 (2) The Oregon Health Authority shall adopt a payment mechanism to pay prohibited
10 entities on a fee-for-service basis for services provided to medical assistance recipients.
11 (3) The authority shall have sole responsibility to pay a prohibited entity under this sec-
12 tion, including for services provided to a member of a coordinated care organization.
13 (4) The authority may not pay a prohibited entity under this section using federal
14 Medicaid funds.
15 (5) In adopting the payment mechanism described in subsection (2) of this section, the
16 authority shall:
17 (a) Update, as necessary, the authority’s billing and claims processing systems; and
18 (b) Ensure that provider enrollment and credentialing requirements for prohibited enti-
19 ties align with existing standards in the state medical assistance program.
20 (6) At least once each biennium, the authority shall conduct a rate analysis to ensure
21 that the rates paid to a prohibited entity under this section are adequate to promote access
22 to reproductive health services.
23 SECTION 3. Section 2 of this 2026 Act applies to claims for payment in the state medical
24 assistance program for services provided by a prohibited entity on or after July 4, 2025, if:
NOTE: Matter in boldfaced type in an amended section is new; matter [italic and bracketed] is existing law to be omitted.

HB 4127
1 (1) The claim is not eligible for federal financial participation; and
2 (2) The prohibited entity has not yet received payment on the claim.
3 SECTION 4. (1) As used in this section:
4 (a) “Coordinated care organization” has the meaning given that term in ORS 414.025.
5 (b) “Prohibited entity” has the meaning given that term in section 2 of this 2026 Act.
6 (2) If a coordinated care organization paid a claim submitted by a prohibited entity be-
7 tween July 4, 2025, and the effective date of this 2026 Act, the coordinated care organization
8 may recover an overpayment on the claim for routine business reasons but may not recover
9 payment on the claim solely on the basis that the prohibited entity is not eligible to receive
10 federal Medicaid funds.
11 SECTION 5. Section 4 of this 2026 Act is repealed on January 2, 2028.
12 SECTION 6. (1) As used in this section:
13 (a) “Medical assistance” has the meaning given that term in ORS 414.025.
14 (b) “Prohibited entity” has the meaning given that term in section 2 of this 2026 Act.
15 (2) The Oregon Health Authority shall implement a grant program to support prohibited
16 entities in covering the costs of providing services to medical assistance recipients. To the
17 extent practicable, the amount of a grant awarded under this section shall be equivalent to
18 the funding amount that a prohibited entity would receive in the authority’s fee-for-service
19 payment system.
20 (3) The authority may not award grants under this section using federal Medicaid funds.
21 SECTION 7. (1) Section 6 of this 2026 Act becomes operative only if a state or federal law
22 is enacted that prohibits a prohibited entity, as defined in section 2 of this 2026 Act, from
23 enrolling in the state medical assistance program.
24 (2) If a state or federal law described in subsection (1) of this section is enacted, section
25 6 of this 2026 Act becomes operative on the effective date of that law.
26 (3) The Oregon Health Authority shall immediately notify the Legislative Counsel if a
27 state or federal law described in subsection (1) of this section is enacted.
28 SECTION 8. This 2026 Act being necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
29 peace, health and safety, an emergency is declared to exist, and this 2026 Act takes effect
30 on its passage.
