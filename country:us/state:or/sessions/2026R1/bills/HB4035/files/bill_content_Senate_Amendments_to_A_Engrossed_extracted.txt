4 “SECTION 6. Section 54, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, is amended to read:
5 “Sec. 54. (1) After the deadline for submission of applications established under section [55 of
6 this 2024 Act] 53, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, the city shall:
7 “(a) Review applications filed for compliance with sections 49 to 59, chapter 110, Oregon Laws
8 2024 [of this 2024 Act].
9 “(b) For each completed application that complies with sections 49 to 59, chapter 110, Oregon
10 Laws 2024 [of this 2024 Act], provide notice to the residents of the proposed site area who were not
11 signatories to the application.
12 “(c) Provide opportunities for public participation in selecting a site, including, at least:
13 “(A) One public comment period;
14 “(B)(i) One meeting of the city’s planning commission at which public testimony is considered;
15 “(ii) One meeting of the city’s council at which public testimony is considered; or
16 “(iii) One public open house; and
17 “(C) Notice on the city’s website or published in a paper of record at least 14 days before:
18 “(i) A meeting under subparagraph (B) of this paragraph; and
19 “(ii) The beginning of a comment period under subparagraph (A) of this paragraph.
20 “(d) Consult with, request necessary information from and provide the opportunity for written
21 comment from:
22 “(A) The owners of each lot or parcel within the site;
23 “(B) If the city does not currently exercise land use jurisdiction over the entire site, the gov-
24 erning body of each county with land use jurisdiction over the site;
25 “(C) Any special district that provides urban services to the site; and
26 “(D) Any public or private utility that provides utilities to the site.
27 “(2) An application filed under this section must:
28 “(a) Be completed for each property owner or group of property owners that are proposing an
29 urban growth boundary amendment under sections 49 to 59, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024 [of this
30 2024 Act];
31 “(b) Be in writing in a form and format as required by the city;
32 “(c) Specify the lots or parcels that are the subject of the application;
33 “(d) Be signed by all owners of lots or parcels included within the application; and
34 “(e) Include each owner’s signed consent to annexation of the properties if the site is added to
35 the urban growth boundary.

1 “(3) If the city has received approval from all property owners of such lands, in writing in a
2 form and format specified by the city, the governing body of the city may select an application and
3 the city shall adopt a conceptual plan as described in section 55, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024,
4 [of this 2024 Act] for all or a portion of the lands contained within the application.
5 “(4) A conceptual plan adopted under subsection (3) of this section must include findings iden-
6 tifying reasons for inclusion of lands within the conceptual plan and reasons why lands, if any,
7 submitted as part of an application that was partially approved were not included within the con-
8 ceptual plan.”.

13 “SECTION 10. Section 11 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of sections 49 to
14 59, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024.
15 “SECTION 11. (1) The City of Woodburn, when adding a site to the city’s urban growth
16 boundary under sections 49 to 59, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024:
17 “(a) Is not required to comply with the selection conditions in section 50 (1)(b)(D)(ii) or
18 (iii), chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024.
19 “(b) Is not required to solicit submission of applications or otherwise comply with section
20 50 (1)(e) or 53, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, provided that the city deliver a notice to
21 Marion County, the Department of Land Conservation and Development and any special dis-
22 trict providing urban services within the city’s urban growth boundary upon receipt of an
23 application.
24 “(c) May demonstrate need under section 52, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, as of the
25 date of the delivery of the notice under paragraph (b) of this subsection.
26 “(d) May approve a site upon receipt of an application without subjecting the site to any
27 of the processes under section 54 (1), chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024.
28 “(e) Is not required to adopt a binding conceptual plan under section 50 (1)(g), 54 (3) or
29 55 (2), chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, before adding a site to the city’s urban growth
30 boundary under paragraph (f) of this subsection.
31 “(f) May select a site application that complies with section 54 (2), chapter 110, Oregon
32 Laws 2024, and, following submission under paragraph (h)(A) of this subsection and approval
33 under subsection (2) of this section, add the site to the city’s urban growth boundary.
34 “(g) For a site added under paragraph (f) of this subsection, shall adopt a master plan
35 that, following submission under paragraph (h)(B) of this subsection and approval under
36 subsection (2) of this section, must be applied to the site upon annexation of the site. The
37 master plan must:
38 “(A) Meet any requirements applicable to a conceptual plan under section 55 (3), chapter
39 110, Oregon Laws 2024; and
40 “(B) Require no fewer than 600 residential units to be developed on the site.
41 “(h) Shall have the city’s site selection reviewed, in lieu of the approval procedures set
42 forth in section 57, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, by submitting to the department:
43 “(A) The selected site application and proposed amendment to the city’s urban growth
44 boundary under paragraphs (c) to (f) of this subsection at least 30 days prior to the adoption
45 of the amendment.

1 “(B) A master plan for the site described in paragraph (g) of this subsection at least 30
2 days prior to an annexation hearing for any property included in the site.
3 “(i) Shall comply with an order by the department under subsection (2) of this section.
4 “(2)(a) The department shall review the submittals under subsection (1)(h) of this section
5 for compliance with the provisions of sections 49 to 59, chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, and:
6 “(A) If a submittal substantially complies with the provisions of sections 49 to 59, chapter
7 110, Oregon Laws 2024, issue an order approving the submittal; or
8 “(B) If a submittal does not substantially comply with the provisions of sections 49 to 59,
9 chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024, issue an order remanding the submittal to the city with a
10 specific determination of deficiencies in the submittal and with sufficient detail to identify a
11 specific remedy for any deficiency in a subsequent resubmittal.
12 “(b) The department must issue an order within 30 days of the delivery of a submittal
13 under subsection (1)(h) of this section.
14 “(c) An order issued by the department under this subsection is subject to appeal as de-
15 scribed in section 57 (4), chapter 110, Oregon Laws 2024.
