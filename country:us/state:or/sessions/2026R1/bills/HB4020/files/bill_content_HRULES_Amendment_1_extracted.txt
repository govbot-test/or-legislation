Section 1:
HB 4020-1
(LC 193)
2/2/26 (MNJ/ps)
Requested by Representative BOWMAN
PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO
1 After line 2 of the printed bill, insert:
2 “Whereas state permitting processes are often complex and involve mul-
3 tiple steps, documentation requirements and agency reviews; and
4 “Whereas the absence of defined timelines and performance expectations
5 can create uncertainty for applicants attempting to plan construction, hiring,
6 financing or other time-sensitive activities; and
7 “Whereas without consistent performance measures related to permit
8 timeliness, agencies and policymakers lack the information necessary to
9 identify bottlenecks, evaluate delays and consider process improvements;
10 now, therefore,”.
11 Delete lines 4 through 21 and insert:
12 “SECTION 1. Sections 2 and 3 of this 2026 Act are added to and
13 made a part of ORS chapter 183.
14 “SECTION 2. (1) This section applies to the following agencies:
15 “(a) The Department of Environmental Quality.
16 “(b) The Department of Transportation.
17 “(c) The Department of State Lands.
18 “(d) The State Department of Energy.
19 “(e) The State Department of Geology and Mineral Industries.
20 “(2) When an agency listed in subsection (1) of this section denies
21 an application for a permit, as defined in ORS 183.700, the agency shall
1 specify the statutory or regulatory authority justifying the denial and
2 provide the applicant a guide on how to contest the denial and the
3 applicable time limitations for contesting the denial.
4 “SECTION 3. (1) This section applies to the following agencies:
5 “(a) The Department of Environmental Quality.
6 “(b) The Department of Transportation.
7 “(c) The Department of State Lands.
8 “(d) The State Department of Energy.
9 “(e) The State Department of Geology and Mineral Industries.
10 “(2) No later than July 1, 2029, an agency listed in subsection (1) of
11 this section shall develop or identify a performance measure as pro-
12 vided in ORS 291.110 that measures the timeliness of processing and
13 issuance of permits and renewals of permits. The performance meas-
14 ure must be accompanied by a description of what slows processing
15 and issuance or renewal of permits and what steps in the processing
16 of permits take the most time. The performance measure may be ac-
17 companied by suggestions for statutory and other changes that could
18 accelerate the timelines.
19 “(3) On or after July 1, 2039, an agency may reevaluate the useful-
20 ness of the performance measure required by subsection (2) of this
21 section and may discontinue using the performance measure.
22 “SECTION 4. Section 3 of this 2026 Act is repealed on January 2,
23 2040.
24 “SECTION 5. This 2026 Act takes effect on the 91st day after the
25 date on which the 2026 regular session of the Eighty-third Legislative
26 Assembly adjourns sine die.”.
