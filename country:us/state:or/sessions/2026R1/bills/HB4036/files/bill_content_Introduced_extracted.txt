Section 1:
House Bill 4036
Introduced and printed pursuant to House Rule 12.00. Presession filed (at the request of House Interim Committee
on Housing and Homelessness for Representative Pam Marsh)
SUMMARY
The following summary is not prepared by the sponsors of the measure and is not a part of the body thereof subject
to consideration by the Legislative Assembly. It is an editor’s brief statement of the essential features of the
measure as introduced. The statement includes a measure digest written in compliance with applicable readability
standards.
Digest: This Act sets up a bond fund to preserve low-cost housing and requires OHCS to report
on low-cost housing barriers. (Flesch Readability Score: 63.8).
Establishes the Preserve Affordable Homes for Oregon Fund to deposit Article XI-Q bonds to
be used to preserve affordable housing at risk of loss.
Requires the Housing and Community Services Department to, with input from stakeholders,
report to the interim committees of the Legislative Assembly relating to housing on or before De-
cember 1, 2026, on legal and policy barriers to affordable housing production.
Declares an emergency, effective on passage.
1 A BILL FOR AN ACT
2 Relating to affordable housing; creating new provisions; amending ORS 456.559 and 456.824 and
3 section 1, chapter 630, Oregon Laws 2025; and declaring an emergency.
4 Be It Enacted by the People of the State of Oregon:
5 SECTION 1. Section 2 of this 2026 Act is added to and made a part of ORS 456.766 to
6 456.828.
7 SECTION 2. (1) The Preserve Affordable Homes for Oregon Fund is established in the
8 State Treasury, separate and distinct from the General Fund. Interest earned on moneys in
9 the Preserve Affordable Homes for Oregon Fund shall be credited to the fund.
10 (2) The fund consists of moneys deposited into the fund from bonds issued by the State
11 Treasurer pursuant to ORS 286A.035 for the Housing and Community Services Department
12 to be used for affordable housing preservation.
13 (3) Moneys in the fund are continuously appropriated to the department to:
14 (a) Acquire, construct, remodel, repair, equip or furnish affordable housing at risk of
15 loss, as described in ORS 456.824 (2), that is or will be owned or operated, as those terms are
16 defined in ORS 458.480, by the State of Oregon for the purpose of providing affordable hous-
17 ing;
18 (b) Pay the costs incurred by the department to administer the fund; and
19 (c) Pay bond-related costs, as defined in ORS 286A.816.

23 stakeholders, including affordable housing providers, finance entities and tenant advocates
24 and that:
25 (1) Evaluates and offers improvements to any state laws or practices, policies or rules
26 of the department that negatively impact the efficiency and cost of providing affordable
27 housing; and

3 SECTION 4. ORS 456.824 is amended to read:
4 456.824. The Housing and Community Services Department shall establish and maintain an af-
5 fordable housing preservation program to coordinate affordable housing preservation efforts and
6 incentivize the analysis and preservation of affordable housing, including publicly supported housing
7 and manufactured dwelling parks. Under the program, the department shall:
8 (1) Collect and maintain data from local, state and federal sources on affordable housing in this
9 state, including the status of existing affordability restrictions and rental assistance contracts at
10 publicly supported housing.
11 (2) Identify categories of affordable housing at risk of loss, including:
12 (a) Properties with expiring federal rent assistance contracts;
13 (b) Properties with expiring affordability restrictions;
14 (c) Properties with affordability restrictions in physical or financial distress; and
15 (d) Manufactured dwelling parks at risk of sale or closure.
16 (3) Take actions to promote housing stability, for residents of:
17 (a) Affordable housing that is at risk of loss or that has been recently lost; or
18 (b) Residents of manufactured dwelling parks or marinas at risk of sale or closure.
19 (4) Establish and periodically update evaluation criteria for affordable housing preservation [in-
20 vestments].
21 (5) Identify and aggregate available sources of funding, including moneys available from federal
22 and private grants or programs, appropriations by the Legislative Assembly, existing funds of the
23 department or financing available from Article XI-Q bond proceeds under ORS 286A.824 or 458.480
24 to 458.490 or section 2 of this 2026 Act or proceeds of other bonds issued by the State Treasurer,
25 and use such sources of funding to implement preservation [investments for] of affordable housing in
26 accordance with the established evaluation criteria.
27 (6) Regularly analyze operating expenses of the existing portfolio of publicly supported housing
28 that is funded by the department to inform the department’s underwriting standards for financing
29 the construction of new and the preservation of existing publicly supported housing and to inform
30 forecasts of preservation funding needs.
31 (7) Regularly update and publish an affordable housing preservation strategy framework report
32 that includes information identified in subsections (1) and (2) of this section and forecasts the fi-
33 nancial need to preserve affordable housing.
34 (8) Execute the duties of the department under ORS 456.766 to 456.828.
