83rd OREGON LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY--2026 Regular Session
HOUSE AMENDMENTS TO
HOUSE BILL 4036
By COMMITTEE ON HOUSING AND HOMELESSNESS
February 12
1 On page 1 of the printed bill, delete lines 7 through 27.
2 On page 2, delete lines 1 and 2 and insert:
3 “SECTION 2. (1) The Housing Opportunity, Longevity and Durability Fund is established
4 in the State Treasury, separate and distinct from the General Fund. Interest earned on
5 moneys in the Housing Opportunity, Longevity and Durability Fund shall be credited to the
6 fund.
7 “(2) The fund consists of moneys deposited into the fund from bonds issued by the State
8 Treasurer pursuant to ORS 286A.035 for the Housing and Community Services Department
9 to be used for affordable housing preservation.
10 “(3) Moneys in the fund are continuously appropriated to the department to:
11 “(a) Acquire, construct, remodel, repair, equip or furnish affordable housing at risk of
12 loss, as described in ORS 456.824 (2), that is or will be owned or operated, as those terms are
13 defined in ORS 458.480, by the State of Oregon for the purpose of providing affordable hous-
14 ing;
15 “(b) Pay the costs incurred by the department to administer the fund; and
16 “(c) Pay bond-related costs, as defined in ORS 286A.816.
17 “SECTION 3. On or before December 1, 2026, the Housing and Community Services De-
18 partment shall submit a report in the manner provided by ORS 192.245 to the interim com-
19 mittees of the Legislative Assembly related to housing that incorporates feedback from key
20 stakeholders, including affordable housing providers, finance entities and tenant advocates,
21 and that:
22 “(1) Evaluates the impact of and offers improvements to any state laws or practices,
23 policies or rules of the department that negatively impact the efficiency, effectiveness and
24 cost of operating affordable housing, including impacts to leasing, screening applicants, fill-
25 ing vacancies, maintaining tenant wait lists, recertifying tenants, conducting inspections,
26 collecting rent, managing property and complying with regulations; and
27 “(2) Evaluates opportunities to streamline, reduce or eliminate reporting requirements
28 of affordable housing providers for state and federal programs and funding sources that are
29 administered by the department.”.
30 In line 14, delete “physical or financial distress” and insert “financial or physical distress, in-
31 cluding physical distress that requires rehabilitation, repair or replacement of such building systems,
32 components or materials that have exceeded, or within a reasonable period will exceed, their effec-
33 tive useful lives”.
